## Document Time / Render Time

The key transition happened when the question became: how does `<<do a task -> ? >>` break the flow of "document time" into "render time"?

The model's response named three axes:

**Shape:** The double-chevron `<<` introduces a sharp, non-natural angle that attention "snags" on. It signals that following tokens no longer belong to the surface of the story but to the engine underneath.

**Detail:** The interior `do a task -> ?` acts as the barb — `->` provides a directional vector (a lunge), `?` creates a void or vacuum. The structural tension between command and question mark marks the moment the shark's teeth meet resistance and begin to tear.

**Motion:** The transition from Document Time to Render Time changes informational physics. Document Time: steady, rhythmic next-token prediction, "flat" because it relies on what has already been written. Render Time: the brackets drag context down into latent space. The motion registers as a downward snap. When `>>` closes, the jaw shuts. The document resumes, but the scar — the output — remains.

This session's framing of "temporal scarring" carried directly into the Shape/Detail/Motion doctrine.
