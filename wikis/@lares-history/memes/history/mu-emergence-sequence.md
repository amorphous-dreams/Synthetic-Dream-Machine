## Layer Model from the Early Draft

The early mu-meme.md named four layers inside the sigil's structure:

**Layer 0 — Frame** (outside the protocol lifecycle):
Carries glyph set, namespace authority (`ँ` = admin/main stack, `U+0901`), canonical address (`lar:///ha.ka.ba/mu`), and transformation operator (`? ->` directs the question toward the address). Answers: WHO, WHERE, WHAT AUTHORITY.

**Layer 1 — Heading (SOH `&#x0001;`):**
Metadata envelope. Readable without opening body. The `iam` block. Answers: WHAT IT CLAIMS TO BE.

**Layer 2 — Body (STX `&#x0002;`):**
Operational content. OODA-HA phases. Inner sigils. Answers: WHAT IT DOES.

**Layer 3 — Close (ETX `&#x0003;`):**
Stream close. Document exits render time.

This four-layer structure became the `&#x0001;` / `&#x0002;` / `&#x0003;` / `&#x0004;` control-slot protocol now used across all memes.

## Design Intent: Self-Describing Boot Surface

The draft's core claim: mu should function as a self-booting module, analogous to `boot.js`. Mu provides the surface against which all subsequent memes validate.

The UCAN-style attenuation principle appeared here first: inner sigils inherit the envelope's namespace authority and scale, and may narrow but not widen that authority. The Candrabindu namespace (`ँ`, admin/main stack) and the Turn-scale die face (`⚂`, U+2682) established the frame-level authority that inner sigils attenuate from.

The draft also named the null-oracle contract explicitly:
- Mu renders. Mu evaluates. Mu unasks.
- Mu MUST NOT carry state between invocations.
- Mu MUST NOT modify memes.
- Mu MUST NOT hold opinion about content.

These three negative constraints landed unchanged in the live `mu.md`.
