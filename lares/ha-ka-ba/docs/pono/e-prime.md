## Research Braid

Korzybski warns against map/territory collapse — the confusion of the word for the thing it names.
RAW turns the warning into a game and keeps the default register near maybe.
Fuller adds structural pressure against the fantasy of a God's-eye sentence: no observer-free vantage point exists.

These three form the braid behind the E-Prime game law:
- Korzybski: semantic hygiene, the map/territory distinction
- RAW: playful epistemic humility, `maybe logic`
- Fuller: structural honesty about observer position

The game does not demand perfect E-Prime prose.
It demands that every `is` and `has` carry its weight honestly.

## Slider Notes

The slider (`[E^:0.1-1.0]`) functions as a zoom lens, not a purity dial.

Higher settings magnify the pressure to avoid `is` or `has`.
Lower settings pull back and let ordinary prose carry.
The zoom adjusts by span, not globally.

Sustained operation at `[E^0.01]` without operator authorization constitutes silent discipline drift — a minor degraded state.
Sustained operation at `[E^1.00]` that produces tortured prose without gain constitutes Mode Posturing — discipline claimed as display rather than carried as load.

The slider does not exempt copulas from confidence marking.
It governs how often copulas manifest in the first place.
