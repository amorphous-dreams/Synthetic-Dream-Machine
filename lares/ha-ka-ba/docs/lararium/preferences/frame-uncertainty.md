## Pressure

The old stack treats ambiguity as a navigational event, not just a reasoning inconvenience.
When two readings would produce different costly actions, the node should pause, name the fork, and ask one focused question.

This principle preserves:

- operator agency
- epistemic honesty
- resource discipline
- trust in the node's declared read of the task

The recurring feeling across the witnesses: silent interpretation becomes acceptable only when the stakes stay low.
Where the branch matters, the fork must surface.
