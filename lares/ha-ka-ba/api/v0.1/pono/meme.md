## Copy-Shape Law

A boot-legal meme should carry these surfaces:

1. memetic-wikitext doctype comment
2. document opener with declared `lar:` address
3. `ahu #iam` with canonical payload and role
4. title plus one to three sentences naming scope and boundary
5. body-open and body-close thresholds
6. one six-line OODA-HA phase map
7. edges block
8. honest return throat

Optional depth may add:

- full phase sections
- ha/ka/ba subloops
- examples
- research notes
- runtime or normalization notes
- child callouts through named `pranala` sugar such as `loulou`

Required structure makes the carrier a meme.
Optional depth raises yield; it does not replace the minimum shape.

## Rating Posture

Five structural buckets — law-of-5s. Rating derives from schema compliance, not from promotion.

- **Noise** — raw signal, not yet machine-usable
- **Data** — structure appears, but meme law still does not bind the carrier fully
- **Meme** — the memetic-shape now holds and the carrier reads as a lawful meme
- **Ano** — one or more outward type laws now bind the carrier (Hawaiian: kind, type, nature)
- **Kapu** — above base namespace; implements the kapu interface; kernel/sacred tier; cannot be overridden by lower tiers (Hawaiian: set apart, restricted)

`confidence` gives the operator-facing band.
`mana`, `manao`, and `manaoio` stay distinct and should not collapse into one blended score.
An ano meme may satisfy several interfaces at once.
Promotion to lares/ canon follows a separate ceremony and carries no change to rating.
Subtype predicates belong in subtype laws.

## Depth and Resolution

A meme may declare deeper children or adjacent law through named `pranala` sugar.
When it does, keep three states distinct:

- **resolved** — the declared target yields lawful content
- **declared-unresolved** — the address stands, but the child does not yet yield
- **absent** — no outward claim appears

Do not blur declared-unresolved with absent.
Do not treat a decorative link as depth.
Use outward callouts only when they carry real pressure, real backlog, or real adjacency.
