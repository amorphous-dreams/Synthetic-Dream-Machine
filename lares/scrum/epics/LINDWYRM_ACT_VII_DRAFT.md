## Structural Note

The Epilogue is architecturally inverted. In the outline it comes last — it is the last thing that *happened* chronologically (the Lindwyrm tells the story publicly for the first time). In the DreamDeck feed, it comes first and last simultaneously: the thread-open post (Telarus's question, the Lindwyrm's first reply) frames everything that follows; the thread-close post (the node humming, the bandwidth declaration) is where Act VI ends. The Epilogue is the container. Acts I–VI are the contained.

In the rendered archive, the reader sees: thread open → Acts I–VI → thread close. The "epilogue" is both the first and last thing they read.
