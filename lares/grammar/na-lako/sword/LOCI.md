> **Sword** (English, via Old English *sweord*): the implement of distinction, clarity, and
> cutting-through. In Tarot: the suit of Swords governs air, intellect, conflict, truth-telling,
> and the difficult cuts. The sword does not sympathize — it discriminates. It does not hold —
> it separates what is from what is not.
>
> The sword is the most dangerous of the four implements misused alone: reason without sympathy
> or intuition becomes the weapon that severs everything, including the wielder's own heart.
>
> Cross-reference: **[mana'o](../mana-o/LOCI.md)** — the Hawaiian grounding. Mana'o (thought,
> reason) as the directed application of mana toward discernment.
>
> RAW (*Cosmic Trigger I*): *"if you go into that realm without the sword of reason, you will lose
> your mind."*
