> **Koa** (Hawaiian): *warrior; brave, bold, fearless.* The koa tree (*Acacia koa*) is the
> hardwood of the islands — used for canoes, surfboards, weapons, and bowls. The koa tree grows
> on the mountain slopes, in the rough country. *He koa* — "a warrior." *Koa no ka ʻōpū* —
> "brave of heart/belly." The word carries both the warrior's courage and the material hardness
> of the wood they carry.
>
> The pentacle is the earth suit — grounded, material, present. Valor is not the absence of
> fear; it is the willingness to enter despite fear. Those without the pentacle of valor "stand
> in terror outside the door of Chapel Perilous, trembling and warning all who would enter."
> The koa enters.
>
> The pentacle also maps to the *kahua* — the prepared ground that the warrior stands on.
> Koa + kahua: valor grounded in foundation.
>
> RAW: *"if you are armed with the wand of intuition, the cup of sympathy, the sword of reason
> and the **pentacle of valor**..."*
