This second adventure is about character and setting growth and development. The characters will sketch out a new region, discover a new town, and explore an estate that may become their base of operations.

The PCs will also begin to notice the outlines of two meta-factions:

1. **Ministry** — the meta-faction of order. All those individuals and groups and organizations that believe, more or less, that the Rainbow Lands are alright. That the gods' plan, the Garden Path, the Dream Canopy, call it what you will, is good and working as intended.
2. **Nemesis** — the meta-faction of upheaval. Those who are convinced that something has gone terribly wrong with the Golden Age, that the system is failing, that struggle, change, and possibly revolution are required.

**Will and Testament**

The council of elders met in the primary room.

Everlamps lit the old plastic walls and the stackable injection molded chairs squeaked as they shifted on the standardstone floor.

“Thank you,” sang the synthetic voice of Demoiselle Urlaia, comptroller of commerce, as her bulky steel exo body slid into position at the levitating tabletop.

At this point, it is unclear to which of these two camps either the Sender or the Antagonist may be assigned. That will be up to the referee and the players.

As with the first adventure, the referee can play it out step-by-step with the other players, or pregenerate it. In case you are playing it out together, give a copy of pages XX-33 to XX-43 to all the players at the start of the session to help them co-create the session.

Cocreating a setting, a region, town, estate, or faction for the first time is a game of imagination. Don't worry whether things make perfect sense. That's alright — a world and a story get built two steps forwards, one step back.

#### REGIONAL OMENS

The wise traveler must always look out for signs. Portents. Messages of what is to come, what was, what is to be. There are lessons in the souls and soils we see as we travel. Warnings but also opportunities. Instructions for the questing eye and grasping hand and running foot.

- ⚙ **Within the zone**. Moving from location to adjacent location takes fifteen (15) minutes. Performing a useful action at a location takes an hour (60 minutes). We're abstracting finding the right building and room.
- ⚙ **Within the settlement**. Moving from zone to adjacent zone (or periphery) takes thirty (30) minutes. An action within a zone takes two hours. We're abstracting moving around, finding the location, then the building, etc.
- ⚙ **Within the region**. Moving from district to adjacent district (or settlement) takes two (2) hours. Taking action there consumes four (4) hours. That makes it a whole watch (6 hours) to go to another district and do a thing.

#### RUNNING ESTATES

If cash is the petty coin that hammers and other proles spend to keep themselves in bread, the estate (Σ) is the wealth that gives and keeps giving. The land and bonds, stocks and cryptids, digital daemon tokens and other arcane promises. Illiquid assets.

Estates come in different sizes, and all healthy humans want big wealthy estates. So teaches the Cogflower.

![Estate properties grid (page 57)](images/page_0057_estate_properties_grid.png)

- ⚙ **Humble Estate**. Worth €5,000. Generates a revenue of €5 per week (€5/wk). Enough to scrape by.
- ⚙ **Small Estate**. €10,000. €10/wk. Enough for a whole family of pauperlings.
- ⚙ **Middling Estate**. €50,000. €50/wk. Enough to live comfortably in a middle class neighborhold.
- ⚙ **Fine Estate**. €500,000. €500/wk. Upper class life.
- ⚙ **Grand Estate**. €10M. €10k/wk. The minimum to call oneself a proper baron.

You will find estates are excellent money sinks. After all, the key to more extravagant adventures is a bigger estate! And, clearly, an estate can never be big enough. As the old folk faith song goes, "make number go up to the moon." Academics disagree which moon the song refers to, as there are at least 17 easily visible moons.

**Properties In Their Variety**

One could keep estates abstract. Numbers kept in arcane ledgers by strange daemons and deciphered by the noöspheric ghost essences of the sulphurous priests of Mathic Abaco in their clock-tok zero-one temples.

One could. But that would be awfully trusting. No, far better and safer to trust in soil, for land alone gives birth to that net product which can be called the wealth of humans. Thus spake master Turs Quay Nay Shtra.

There are thus four categories of estate:

- ⚙ **House**. Your castle. Where you live, grow, learn, pray, entertain, shelter, bunker, recover, and revive (if you have a chamber of living waters).
- ⚙ **Property Present**. Lands abutting and controlled by your house, where you can extract maximum revenue from the souls chosen to serve you.
- ⚙ **Property Absent**. Far away lands, administered for you by conniving, untrustworthy agents.
- ⚙ **Property Abstract**. The aforementioned numbers in arcane ledgers. Barely comprehensible. But very low maintenance. Please do not look behind the curtain.

Your starting estate, by the wisdom of the Sender and the generosity of the Departed Sieur, includes a house (see Domus Nova, pXX) and properties present.

#### CASH ASSETS

_Property makes a proper person._ —traditional saying, Yellow Lands

What is property? It is a relationship between subjects and objects, between people and things. The thread that connects care and labor with the world physical. The law that divides scarce resources. The narrative that makes the world useful.

It is how you prove that you exist. It is the shackle that binds you to this Given World. It is the resource that fuels your ambitions.

Every proper rainbowlander knows what cash (€) is. The twice-barred "c". Liquid assets.

- ⚙ €1 = An unskilled human's day wage.

Cash is an abstraction for the different kinds of bills, coins, cards, cheques, and credits that the humans of the Circle Sea Civilizational Megaregion use in their daily truck and barter.

**Spending Cash**

Cash is your most versatile social resource. Credit crystallized. You spend it to buy gear for your adventures. You use it for fines and bribes, food and tickets, hotels and guides. Anything, really.

In play, you can get by without tracking coins and credit when something costs much less than a day's wages.

**Investing Cash**

Crucially, you need cash to become a better person, because the more you are worth, the better you are.

This is obviously true. Everyone says it, even the Mother Machine.

The secret to having a lot of cash is to be very wealthy. The way to become wealthy is to invest your cash in your properties, where it will be fruitful and multiply and come back to you by the mysterious workings of the Invisible Hand. Praise Mohlack (but discreetly)!

**Mortgage**

You can mortgage your properties to get quick cash.

- ⚙ Reduce your revenue by €1/wk and get €500.

It will probably cost €1,000 to pay off a €500 mortgage. Hope you invested that cash well. Like in a profitable trade adventure.

**Revenue**

Revenue (R) is your cash flow before expenses. It's the amount of money you imagine you could have if you were a creature of pure light, who didn't need air or water or food or shelter or a social life.

The simplest source of revenue is your estate, but it can also come from other sources like titles, tributes, tithes, contracts, and other nifty rent-extraction schemes.

Tales say that once, when the Dream Canopy was untattered, every human received a cash each day to consume as they liked. Some say this was a lovely time.

Nowadays, when dragons no longer fuel furnaces, some grovellings and many of the plebeian sort must trade their given time on this Given World for cash. The unskilled and shiftless earn €7 per week, but master servants of earn up to €60 per week or more (p57).

**Expenses**

Expenses (E) is the cash that leaves your accounts every week, like grains of emerald sand pouring through your fingers as you try to hold it together while the jade dragon's visions assail your sanity.

As with revenue, your estate is the source of most ongoing expenses. Your quarters and lifestyle, specialized rooms, rented machinery and more. However, you can incur almost infinite expenses, such as wages for followers, fees for experts, gifts to worthy causes, and other generous status-enhancing efforts.

Fairy stories say how once, when the sky was full of more stars than one could count, humans had no expenses or obligation other than to sing, dance, and make poetry in the Garden of Delights—not to please some distant Lords but to entertain themselves. Other stories say these were not humans but those creatures now called Viles.

**Income**

Income (i) is revenue less expenses. This is how much cash you actually gain per week. Your disposable income.

Revenue (R) - Expenses (E) = income (i)

Go on. Go wild. Spend it on something nice, like a longsword or a classic cinematic vidy or maybe that fancy hat you've wanted for a while.

Income increases your cash pile. If you need a large amount of cash to go on an adventure, you can simply spend some time accumulating cash, let the world-clock wind along, seasons come and go, events happen, and then finally go on your adventure.

**Improvising Places**

It is time to bluster and invent your way out of this situation. For their own good, of course. After all, to improvise is the way of the referee.

Wheel out your oracle, the random die. Start up the imagination engine. Even toss the question back at the players. Let them engage in co-creation. Ask questions, hard and strange questions, to build a vivid place:

- ⚙ What is the one and only thing that defines this lazyplace? What is its stereotype? Of course it is incomplete, incorrect, and always misleading. But somehow also quite true.
- ⚙ What is a trait of this commune? Exaggerate it! What is a thing they oppose? Assign that to their neighbors.
- ⚙ What do these people say about themselves? What secretly defines them? Even they do not know themselves!
- ⚙ How do they describe a minority that dwells among them? What do they ascribe to the nearby others? How are both these ideas wrong? What is a hidden fact about those groups? Again—they may not understand this.
- ⚙ What do they say they want? What do they really need? How are these things different?
- ⚙ What do they not want to see? Remember: there are none so blind as those who will not see.

#### COLORS

_"The color codes protect us from Error."_ —Long Ago catechism, Blue Skull meta-codex 74:147

- ⚙ Yellow. Sacred things, which require divine power and can only be used by the gods and their agents. Forbidden to mere common humans.
- ⚙ Red. Dangerous things, which humans may only use on the fringes of the civilized lands or under supervision. Forbidden to unlicensed humans.
