### ORANGE LAND

_"Party while you live, for we will work when we are dead."_ —traditional Orange saying


> [@OGA, _p._ _156_]

<!-- Our Golden Age, Page 157 -->

The Orange Land provided resources for the human frontier: airmatter, waterstuff, foodwaste. Mechanical mines, automated estates, and postrobotic plantations harvested at scale. The Builders' Councillors worked hard to control these systems. Armies of drones drained swamps and leveled mountains, creating the great valleys of to-day. Orchards blossomed where the Thin Plateau and the Garden Shield were quarried away.

Humans were drawn to this cornucopia of delights, like grasshoppers to the labors of ants. Permitted or not, they were welcomed and began an eternal celebration, a utopia of leisure built on the bodies of ancestors.

Today, the great party cities of the Orange Land draw massive crowds, which studiously ignore the abandoned towns and hollow holds and not-found territories. Many visitors are so overwhelmed by pleasure that they even elect to become ancestors themselves.

Some say their choice is less free than they think.

**Climate**

The great valleys of the Orange Land are blessed by clear skies, warmth, and abundant water from the surrounding highlands. The highlands themselves are harsh, by season cold or hot, dry or wet. As for the coast, there clouds but mock the traveler with promised rain.

**Population**

citizen living: ~0.9 million, citizen dead: ~27.4 mil., duckie (_tei oms duchièmes_): ~2–4 mil., cad: ~0.1 mil.

**Groups**

postmortal laborers: 80%
postmortal security: 5%
citizen freeloaders: 3%
tourist laborers: 9%
tourist tourists: 2%
councilors and agents: 1%

**Languages**

Caddic (command standard), Oranjetic (popular), Orange Circle Kriol (duckie), Topic N (necro-comm)

**Capital**

Official: Orange City
Practical: Oranje of the Sacred Ploughland.

**Government**

Council technocracy. Centrally-planned hedonic-first necro-agrarian socialism. Feuding subaltern resident clans.

**Ideologies**

Orange God: pleasure and justice (administrative), Orange God: cornucopia (popular), The Hitchhiker: digestive (passing through), Machine Beast (unofficial).

**Economy**

The citizens-dead work till they are dust to provide fruits and other delights for the citizens living and hard currency for the council. The second-tier residents, the so-called duckies, toil in a vast grey market to offer those services that the postmortal labor armies cannot.

**Resources**

Fruits, flowers, seeds, grains, woods, bones, sinews, soils, worms, vegetables, tubers, roots, shoots, stems, stones, flesh, organs, beers, liquors, bloods, fluids, and more.

**Means of Production**

All wealth is owned by all the citizens, living and dead, and managed by their selfless representatives, the cads—councilors of ancestors and descendants. The cads police one another to maintain the utopian rites. The cads adjudicate disputes between all categories of citizens and residents.

**Material Conditions**

The citizens-living enjoy the largesse of a necrotechnical utopia, their lives full of pleasures and delights paid for by their body's toil after their mayfly lives end. The duckies take care of themselves in the corners of the great pleasure pyramids and ecstasy edens. The citizens dead are stored in bone beds when not in use.

The public slurry pump rattles and wheezes like a dying dragonscough victim. The hedonic card printer whirrs and clanks impotently. It does not recognize you as a Citizen with an allotted pleasure total.

The billboards mock your rumbling bowels.

**Hedonic Burden Review**

Citizens Living of the Orange Lands do not need money—each is born with a pleasure budget to cover all their needs for the length of a standard efficient lifetime.

The Head Count University, a ministerial level confederate bureau of the Orange Lands governments, keeps strict time and energy records of every citizen to ensure a meaningful level of state function. Citizens Living whose total lifetime consumption would exceed their allocated pleasure, determined according to their pre-calculated needs, are politely encouraged to join the labor force of the Citizens Dead earlier than their statistical natural lifespan might indicate.

**FACTIONS IN AMBER**

_"The political scene in the Orange Lands is dead."_ —Sailing Islands joke

Two groups dominate this land: the Citizens Living, who enjoy the fully-necromantic luxury socialism of the confederated orange polities; and the Citizens Dead, who work until they are dust to support the pleasant lives of their living descendants.

A third class is gladly dominated: the Duckies (a corruption from High Common _Tei Oms Duchièmes_) are foreign residents, in the Orange Lands on limited-term contracts to do jobs the Citizens Dead struggle with. They are permitted to stay and pay taxes as long as they work, but must return home after finishing their tour of duty. Most Duckies have lived in the Orange Lands for five generations or more.

The fourth group is hardly a faction; rather, it is a voluntary benevolent association that gently and politely guides the confederation: the Council of Ancestors and Descendants. They are also known by their acronym, simply as the Cads (not to be confused with the Cats), their philanthropic activities excusing them from the hedonic review and other legal demands.

**Citizens Living**

_"The little citizen lives thrice as long!"_ —popular drinking ditty

Anyone can become a citizen of the Orange Land and enjoy the largesse of this wonderful state, so famous for its musical and party culture, as long as they accept the two peculiar institutions, the Hedonic Burden Review and the Labor Corpse Tax.

Cad legislators vigorously deny these two institutions explain why the contemporary Citizen Living averages just 120 centimeters tall, often too short to make a suitable agricultural worker in undeath. They say it is vile slander that many Orangelanders edit their source codes to reduce their size, since larger citizens consume more, expend their allotted pleasure totals faster and thus enjoy shorter lives.

♟ _Anvalit' Assunta._ The leading living fruit-pop performer and a key part of the Orange Land hedonic propaganda outreach program. Some whisper that she has received six life extensions already for her services to the Land. Others insinuate that she is looking for a way to escape her Agent handlers and flee to the Purple Land.

♟ _Parom Duije III._ A rarity among the Orangelanders, the bourgeois Parom represents a mercantile faction with outland holdings agitating for the right to purchase outland bodies to offset the labor corpse tax. His liberal politicking is viewed with possibly lethal distaste by some ultra-traditionalist Cads.

**Citizens Dead**

A Citizen Living legally becomes a Citizen Dead through the Labor Corpse Tax. Once they die, their body is reanimated and joins a labor corps until it is dust. Their family may generously keep a relic that will not interfere with the citizen's posthumous labors. For example, plantation and mine workers' heads are unnecessary in most occupations, so the family will keep the preserved head in their domestic Cabinet of Ancestries. More often they keep just the face or another keepsake the worker no longer requires.

Unofficially, foreigners, ferals, and other outsiders can also become Citizens Dead of the Orange Land. Some nearby barbarian clans have made a practice of selling their (or more often, their neighbors') dead to the Orange Lands and grown quite wealthy in the process. There's a reason for the Onion Skull saying, "plant skulls for profit, plant alliums for flavor."

♟ _Duger ten Zon._ This Yellowlander Green Nomad scion is spreading cash around the stoic tea houses favored by councilors and Duckies who deny the hedonic imperative. Duger believes their sibling was illegally inducted into the Citizens Dead, and they'll pay to have them (or their head) back. They'll pay even more to have the responsible body-mercs brought to the lawless unincorporated territories between the Orange and Yellow Lands.

♟ _Timox Boscafoc._ Made into a Citizen Dead due to a clerical error, Timox's case has made it all the way to the local Ministerial court. A resurrection for Timox could have drastic consequences for the whole radiant carnibotanic industry and beyond.

**Duckies, Tei Oms Duchièmes**

The foreign residents of the Orange Land are not a faction. They are satisfied to live in a well-ordered society, with a stable pre-cradle to post-grave social infrastructure. Their gratitude is supreme.

Any suggestion that the Duckies outnumber the Citizens Living 3-to-1, or that this could constitute a social problem, is antisocial heresy and punishable by an immediate period of post-mortal labor not shorter than 10 (ten) years.

Some scholarly outlander vidy-personalities have implied that the Duckies view this term (viz. Duckie) as a slur and prefer to be called Uncitizens or the Frees. These vidy-personalities have been banned from entering the Orange Land on pain of 25 years of post-mortal labor.

**THE NECROTECHNOLOGIES**

_The theophysics of this made cosmos give us the observable fact that it is constituted of three levels of matter. The substances of hyle, pneuma, and psyche. In the arcane Builders' language; the ha, ka, and ba. In the low dialects, body, soul, and mind-stuff._ —Aïdu Hylo, _No Matter: A Study of the Tricycle of Existence_, The Free Drift Corrective

If there is any land that has truly embraced the possibilities suggested by triform structure of the Cosmos of the Given World, it is the Orange Land; which has built its entire economy on the society-wide necrotechnology made possible by the tripartibility of each Human 'dividual. As alluded to in Aïdu Hylo's text, these parts of each thing-in-itself (_Ding an sich_) are:

⚙ **Hyle, body, ha.** The underlying substance, devoid of qualities and form, but capable of any quality and form. The stars of the cosmos above, reflected in the bricks of the polis and the flesh of the citizen, mirrored in the mass of the earth below.

⚙ **Pneuma, spirit, ka.** The superior substance, the rarefied body, the fire of creation. The active, generative principle that organizes the world and the human person.

⚙ **Psyche, mind, ba.** The unique formal matter, extant through space and time, the lived material trajectory of a consciousness. Each mind is a shard of the great mind, each sentient being is an eye of the cosmos watching itself. Its relationship with its world is each psychic body's relationship with itself.

**Resurrection Impossible**

It is a dirty secret among most reanimators and portal engineers that a destroyed sentient being can never truly be brought back. Whether that sentience is a mosquito or a mushroom mycelium or a man, their individual sentience is embodied in space and embedded in time, a perfect coherence of hyle, pneuma, and psyche. Once those components are decohered, that entity is ended.

Even if those components are recombined, the result is not a continuity.

It is a new-birth or rather new-creation. A new 'dividual is formed, shaped to resemble the one who was before, but not precisely the same.

When a traveler is translated through a nullportal, they dissipate and a _new them_ takes their place in society. When a warrior dies in battle and is rebuilt in a reanimation vat, that new warrior is a copy who shares their memories and specific skills.

Among lay society, pretending this is not so makes life more tolerable.

And the convenience of fast, easy, painless travel and return from the dead is so alluring.

And yet, once a 'dividual returns to the Cosmos, they are of the Cosmos for their old spiritual eye has closed and a new one must of necessity have opened.

This offers the necrotechnologist or tricycler, as some call themselves, certain opportunities; that is to say, when they reconstitute a 'dividual they can adjust the constituent parts to repair flaws of character, soul, or form, adapting them to better fulfill their role in society or grow into it over time.

_false settlements, bed and banshee houses_


It's not just the Orange Land that is dotted with ghost orchards and hollow plantations and robot cottages, but they are most common there.

The weary traveler follows a sign, spots hearthsmoke, and expects a bed and bath, rest and repose. Lights welcoming wink, tended trees and furrowed fields invite. Closer, figures go about their duties, smells tempt the palate, the circle of life seems unbroken.

Yet something rings hollow. No spirit stirs. No mind inquires. Men and beasts alike are false, machines of flesh, programmed to simulate social life. Survivors of masters gone to futility, dead to ennui, died out while the gods looked away.

Still, the biomachines continue. Ceaseless as the suns.

1. Tasked with marketing a perfect house of the future, they upkeep the half-ruined place, try to sell it.
2. The masters left on a journey a century ago and the falscher butlers have been keeping it livable since.
3. The abmortal minister who retired to a little cottage, but felt lonely in the empty village of her youth and brought synthetic shells to simulate a vanished era.
4. A plague killed all the humans here, but the quotas kept coming at the Adminimind900®, so it animated these plastic people to keep up.
5. The withered people are still there, become Megas, hooked to nöosphere drips in their cellars, their day jobs taken over by falscher clones of themselves.
6. They are not falscher ghost humans, but ferals without mind jewels, who settled here illegally.

**Windling Valleys**

_ghost traps, voice harvesters_


But where did the voices go, the minds chased out of all these mute places? Most faded, many translated into the noösphere, and some—some made other stranger attempts at immortality. One group that still sings their timelocked songs to this day are the windlings, minds trapped ghost-like in the carving of highland rocks and sculpting of stuckforce membranes to channel air and light, giving them voice when the catabatic winds blow.

They mostly have little to say to interest a modern human, complaining about the price of groceries or grousing about their incomprehensible politics of amusement, but their wind-voices, though annoying, are often used to guide travelers in the underpeopled Highland Provinces and Amber Territories.

**THE SHALLOW SEA AND THE SAILING ISLANDS**

_"The Sea is a desert, all life within it false. Those who eat_

_fruit of the Sea lose their minds to the Lightless Unlords. Turn aside from the Sea, that it would not eat your soul."_ —Councilorleit Veyelhuit 5-Perimeter, _Towards a Renewed Orchard_ (Flux Dandelion IV)

Some say. Some. Ah, that lazy, sneaky word. Some may hide so many. Some may hide hypocrites and heretics, rebels and ruiners, corruptors, devastators, all those who turn their eyes and hearts away from good Path and wise Council! And there may be so many now that to say Some should hardly suffice, but we shall not say Many, for that would be to breach the Act of the Disavowal of the Unreconstituted Provinces, and such a breach would see us undone, become as unconscious as an undine.

But surely, surely, the Council and the City must note that though our popular speech of the Citizens Living and the dream tongue of the Citizens Dead may have a thousand song-names for the orchards and the streams and the delights provided by Hesam [Hedonic Satisfaction Ministry –Ed], it has no songs (Hesam). But it has no song-names for the mute places, the places fallen away from the Garden Path, the places sunk to corruption and slow decay, and least for those most-sunk, those reclaimed when the Sea consumed the Perisea.

How without song-names are ministers and magisters to deal with such despondent places, which may exist, I do not claim they do, without a word for them? How can one resolve the challenge of a hush-where, a mute place that no longer speaks, that cannot be spoken of?

The voices of the gods, the whispers of the noösphere, may be stilled in the hush-wheres, but to the old-minded the stones still have dirges to share. If we listen and conduct these words, are we in breach of ADUP?

Why does Oranjetic have no longer a word for Sea?

**Shallow Sea and the Lost Perisea**

_"Geohistorical heresy holds that the Sea once was smaller."_ —Anti-Council Tract on the Sea Pirates

Long Long Ago, prior to the Rainbow of Lands, prior to the Garden of Panhumanity, prior to priority, the Builders excavated a great bowl in the world, whose name is now taboo but was then Cradle Crater and before that _Volyi Kraatr_. This they filled with waters and air, creating the Settlement Area Designated (SAD), while the rest of the world was made habitable.

This Shallow Sea was the Perisea, the Great Plain of First

Garden, the Eden of Reeds, benevolently given to the humans by the lords of the cetaceans, the Undines, for their fruiting. But the humans performed a Forgotten Deed and the Perisea was drowned by the Circle Sea.

No, it was to protect humans from the Builders' worldsculpting magics, the terrifying buildertech, that the Circle Sea drowned this original land, creating the water-desert as a sacred domain for the cetaceans and the Undines who now lock away these unusable powers.

Then was written the Sufficient Water® Myth to help humans to fear the Sea: "Once, the Lords of the Dream Canopy created Sufficient Water® for the Garden. However, the first humans rebelled and sailed to wrest the Eye from the Lords. The Lords, in their mercy, created Rising Water® and flooded the first settlements and made strong the currents and great the distances, throwing the first humans against the thin air that they might gasp and flounder to amuse the Lords."

Good Citizens and Humans should have no word for Sea.

All who dwell in the Sea must be Inhuman. So says the Council. So says the Minister. So says Mother Machine.

**The Truerooted Islands**

Few islands are truerooted in the Soil beneath the Sea.

There is of course the Eye of God, reserved for Lords and Phylakes and the Purest of Humans certified by the Authority, but that is not the domain of any land. Here, in the Shallow Sea, only Heroes Haven has that massed connection, and thus the Citizens' Dead militia walked across the seabed to its roots, ascended its shores, and ate its foul terrorist anti-citizen rebels, and reaffirmed its eternal and unchanging status as a Council Province. **The Tensefoot Islands**

Tethered by ropes of living chitin, chains of sungwood, and tendons of shipmetal, the tensefoot islands are moored to great crawly anchors. They may move about, but only slowly, at the bidding of the Undines. Those who would dwell on these half-Built islands should supplicate the Undines and other aquatic daemons to protect them from the vagaries of nature and the depredations of land-born peacemakers. Antansible Beach is the largest such island-town in the Sailing Islands, but others may hide on the East Ridge.

**The Megachelonian Isles**

It is rumored that the Undines shepherd them, forming a watery constellation that amplifies their song-signal into the Void.

**WHISTLER'S WATCHTOWER AND PENNYBURST CRATERVALE**

If the Orange Land is the beautiful, delicious, enlightened fruit of civilization, its edge, where the South road to the delights of the East once led, is bitter and strange. A thorny land to guard the sweetness of life, say the Recollectors.

Yet even to such strange places the traveler, the adventure-seer, will endeavor.

**Whistler's Watchtower**

_ghost arcology, automatic vertical village_


A living tower, an arcology from Long Long Ago, _betoniste casalbéres_ rising into the thinnest air, sucking light and moisture and vital force, it remains a vibrant golem town, full of radiation ghosts, on the edge of the Orange Lands. Designated an autonomous cultural area, it is run under its own laws and does not contribute citizens dead to the confederation. It is the major fabricator of oldtech autosurgeons in the Rainbowlands and a protected area.

**The Angel Villages**

_cera-fungal cliff villages, oldtech fabricators_


Most of the arcology's wide-sprawling heartwood is dead, a gnarled labyrinth of support struts, desiccated ducts and petrified passageways. Clustered on cerafungal brackets on the southern extensions of the tower, the villages offer a glimpse into the simple lives of Long Ago humans. The villagers live simple, hard-working lives, tending their hanging gardens, killing parasites, and protecting their arcology.

The villagers are called angels because they are all androgyne and sexless. They do not reproduce; new villagers are brought once a year by the sister servitors.

**The Sister Servitors**

_buildertech abbey, institutional mother machine_


In the arcology's northern buds, the sister servitors tend to the Watchtower's germline, watching over the stock of angels and protecting them in accordance with the Great Plan.

They are not able to fully articulate the plan, as it is embedded in their intuitive subroutines. As they put it, "they know when they're following the plan when they're following the plan."

The sister servitors maintain great birthing banks of wombs and gonads sustained in biomechanical nutrient baths. With these, they produce great numbers of new humans. At birth, they divide them into three groups:

- The breeding population. Those with the best germ lines are sent to the anthropozoo, at the heart of the arcology, where they are kept in a realistic human environment to preserve their way of life.
- The settler population. The largest group. Bundled into sustainer cradles and shipped to the High Plateau Settlement Facility by armored hovergolems.
- The angel populations. Surgically desexed with their organs used to replenish the birthing banks. The angels are then sent to the angel villages, to restock the arcology caretakers' numbers.

The servitors have received no critique of their work from the High Plateau Settlement Organization for over two thousand years. They are sure they are doing a good job.
