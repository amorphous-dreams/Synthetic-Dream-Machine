This first adventure is about exploring the world and meeting its people. Though there are conflicts, combat is not necessary—the PCs can talk, deceive, hide, flee, trade and more. Three NPCs will develop through play:

1. The **Sender** — the PCs' mutual acquaintance. They trigger the adventure, but remain off-screen.
2. The **Contact** — the Sender's assistant serves as a guide in a strange new location. They can also serve as bumbling comic relief.
3. The **Antagonist** — the Sender's opponent. Not directly hostile to the PCs, but in competition with them for the plot device: the Sender's holy clavis.

The plot device, **the holy clavis**, is a key that unlocks the location of the next adventure, the Hollow House.

**Vidycaster Moods**

The governor steepled all dozen of her elegant, manicured fingers. She studied them, chest rising in the cyclical parasympathetic breathing rhythm taught by the schools of socioeconomic engineering.

Body and soul aligned once more, her amber eyes fixed on the worker (H.G2).

“The silver ship ... spoke ... to you.”

“I ... in the head ... a voice, like when Father Builder or Mother Sky ...”

“To you,” she cut through their babble, “To a category two hammer.”

The referee can pregenerate events, connections, and messages, or discover the adventure step-by-step with the other players, rolling on tables as they go. All the players should have access to pages XX-10 to XX-15 from the start of the session to help them invent their PCs. Building a world together can be more engaging for the players and less work for the referee.

The PCs don't need deep backstories or personalities. These can develop organically through play, as the group discovers how they respond in different circumstances. Treat odd results or effects as creative challenges. For example, a warrior who fails a save and flees the giant doom spider machine might suffer from arachnophobia ... after that encounter, anyway.

Now, a vignette to set expectations.

“The voice didn’t ask ...”

The governor cocked her head to the side in that strange way the watchers had, the way that activated the second sight.

“No, I suppose it did not,” she murmured.

Her silence stretched. The worker (H.G2) fidgeted, uncomfortable in their polite arms. They must have felt weak and puny without their exogolem.

Finally, she unfolded to her full nine heads’ height. “Come,” she said mildly, “You will translate for the silver ship and we shall see what Mind is behind all this.”

Her hand stroked the worker (H.G2)’s neck and her sixth finger overrode their personality barrier. Without volition, like a recuser’s megaservant, the worker (H.G2) rose and guided her to the beach where the silver ship rested on the pearl and amber shore.

Encounters serve as:
- Local flavor. They build up the location. Provide a layer of color, action, and theme.
- Sometimes, as obstacles. Requiring the PCs to spend resources to continue or alter their path.
- Occasionally, as challenges. Offering resistance to the PCs if they do not alter their plans.
- Rarely, as threats. Hunting, ambushing, or attacking the PCs unless they escape.

#### GOING PLACES

Rainbowlands adventures are about "going there and back again" without dwelling on researching, booking, outfitting, and packing for travel. We want to unfold the big map, move the party token and plant a flag at each new location. This section provides rules for turning cash into character options, an overview of travel modes, and some event and encounter generators to make travel a little spicier if it starts to drag.

**TICKET DICE**

You’re not interested in how an atomic train runs or how to properly stow cargo in an airship’s sinewy hold. You’re a genteel tourist of taste and, purportedly, means! That’s why we’re introducing three flavors of **six-sided ticket dice**.

**Comfort Dice**

When you need pampering. Each comfort die provides +2 temporary life after a good night’s sleep. You may also permanently spend a comfort die to:
1. Reroll a defeat roll. Your hearty breakfast cushioned that blow.
2. Take a short rest, then recover 1d6 x 2 life.
3. Take a long rest, then remove an extra burden _and_ gain 1d6 temporary life.
4. Acquire a civilized item, such as a nice suit or comfy pillow.

**Protection Dice**

When you need insurance. Each protection die provides +1 to saves against damage to you or your property. You may also permanently spend a security die to:
1. Reroll a reaction roll. Your armature may frighten off would-be attackers.
2. Turn an enemy's critical hit into a regular hit.
3. Wait 1d4 days, then receive a copy of a lost item (terms and conditions apply).
4. Acquire a combat item.

**Speed Dice**

When you need to go-go. Each speed die provides a +2 to chases and races. You may also permanently spend a speed die to:
1. Avoid an encounter before rolling reactions.
2. Roll initiative a 2nd time and get an extra turn this round.
3. Safely escape a fight or catch someone who got away.
4. Acquire an alternate means of transport or travel, such as a drug mule or bicycle.

You can also use ticket dice at your destination, so long as you spend them within the week or before your next trip. At a destination, you can also permanently spend any remaining ticket dice to reroll a carousing roll.

**Buying Ticket Dice**

You buy a **ticket for your week’s travel and accommodation** in advance. Pricier tickets tend to give more dice. Choose dice types at purchase. Dice cannot be changed after your journey begins. All prices per caput (per head). Dice cannot be transferred between tickets. New tickets must be bought every week. If you have experience problems, contact the central human travel bureau [~~restricted~~ violet-coded after the televidy unsanitary incident of Void Lotus XIX].

#### THE LIST OF ALL HUMANS

_"... Circle Sea power groups consider all close-to-baseline sentient and soulful post-humans as effectively human and possessing the full spectrum of rights attendant to a soul-body-personality triad. This includes retro-humans, dwarfs, half-elfs, halflings, quarterlings, and half-orcs."_
—Ten Long Ago, _A Guide for the Free Visitor to the Caste Oligarchies of the Circle Sea_, Violet University Press, Wind Hyacinth II

You voyage to meet strange people and their ways, do you not? No? No matter, you notice them all the same, all the humans in their ragged finery and strange shapes.

Of old, the gods divided all sentient creatures of this Given World into two groups. Those protected by the Builders’ Blessing, the humans. Those cast out, fair game called beasts. Each human was blessed with a metaphysical jewel in their head. This jewel, called canopic, recorded their mind and soul and dreaming, and made possible eternal rebirth within the Garden.

We need not dwell on how the gods’ folly poisoned relations between humans and beasts.

In these later days, with the Garden’s Dream Canopy torn holefull, with the jewels of rebirth rare treasures rather than ubiquitous organs, all humans must live as though they are condemned to die, like mere beasts of the land. After all, what human living would crack their skull asunder like an egg and sift through their thinkmatter for the threads of biomagical jewelry?

The modernists of the novel sacred-industrial societies would equate the blessing of humanity with the ability of a creature to embrace the coin and commerce of Good Mohlack. The payment of tax and the purchase of credit as the measure of the man.

Yet, traditional communities around the Circle Sea still gather each night to recite the List of All Humans—the pananthropy of the canopic form, to smooth their resurrections should they die before they wake.

In fact, the List of Humans is a combination of two prior lists; that of the _Six Canopic Categories_, which is reliably dated to the A Priori Intermediate Period (2), and the _Eight Manifest Forms_, which are probably best known from the Silver Age of the Heretical Modernisation Empire. The two lists were more perfectly combined in the Revelation of the First High Intern during the TwentyThird Dynastic Stabilization of the Mother Machine (estimated XVIII cycles of years ago).

#### THE 768 HUMAN LANGUAGES

_Human cultivars develop new languages as horn beetles develop new headgear. After initial experiments in reducing this inefficiency, the Dream Canopy concluded that linguistic and cultural creativity are a necessary condition for Garden human well-being. The babel was therefore discontinued, except on a need-to-use basis._
— fragment, ruined Temple of Involuntary Speech

As you voyage, one of the things you notice is that all these humans and oldtech devices and leftover daemons and stuttering ferals and ghosts of lost times talk, but talk strangely.

The Circle Sea is thick with languages, dialects, jargons, and slangs. Some say this is by design. Who can say? Well, the Mouths of the Babbling God (pseudoheretical) say it is for the benefit of humans, that they be not confused by Hubris or Nemesis.

Fortunately, neither player nor referee needs to perfectly detail this babel of communication. It is enough to have a sense of how it sounds and feels and looks.

**What [How] Good Is Language?**

Once, all humans were born with a language spider linked to their cranial jewel. This ensured all humans could commune perfectly and follow the Builders' instructions flawlessly. It made the distinction between humans and beasts very easy: those who did not speak, the voiceless, were beasts.

Today, with the radiation of languages, even a language spider is no longer enough and beasts may mingle among humans unseen. Tlumacs and other daemons provide sufficient understanding, but fluency in this time of linguistic efflorescence demands study — an expensive pursuit and proof of nobility and goodness.

Regardless, the traveler will usually find themselves in one of three situations:

1. **Mute** or **Unlanguaged.** There is no shared language. A human tries to talk to an elephant, an engineered urk to an elfer mossman, a sentient ant colony to a plastic golem, a book to an illiterate feral. Misunderstandings are rife. To charm another at this level of linguistic incompetence is nearly impossible, to convey mere facts and concepts a struggle.
   For critical communication, PCs must roll a test. Failure may provoke (roll d6): (1) hostility, (2–3) derision, (4–5) laughter, or (6) pity.
2. **Competent.** There is a shared language. Whether this is through study or tlumac daemon intermediation, a living translator or an implanted language. Communication proceeds normally, if without brilliance. Charming an interlocutor requires strange pheromones, inspired luck, obfuscatory hallucinations, or sheer magic.
3. **Natural** or **Native Fluency.** The speaker has invested in their language and it shows. Natives trust native speakers more. Deception, intimidation, even persuasion are easier.
   PCs must have a relevant trait to claim this level of linguistic gilding: oratory, politics, rhetoric, theater, or an upbringing in a mytho-poetic rustic rendition of what the culture holds near and dear (folk hero).

**Learn Languages Faster**

Few humans in this golden age have the patience, opportunity, or capacity to learn a language the old way with tutor golems and flash scales.

- **Tlumac daemons** are wisps of sentience, simulacra of personhood trapped in physical items and bound to service their owners or lease-holders. Most travelers rent the services of a tlumac daemon when they visit a foreign parochial community for €1d4 per week (depending on the rarity of the language). Buying a servitor tlumac costs around €100 for a 5-language model or €500 for a 10-language type. Typical tlumac forms (d6): (1) amulet, (2) earring, (3) necklace, (4) earworm, (5) jawbud, (6) third eye.
- **Injectable brain-rewiring** is the safe and affordable solution for those who need to acquire linguistic skill fast. **The Good Common Speech[es]**

Every traveler will be aware that there are hundreds of human languages in the Circle Sea and many more thousands of beastly languages beyond. Fortunately, every educated traveler knows that little more is required than an excellent apprehension of the Common Speech—the so-called Sermo Comun of the Ministerials.

Sadly, Old Common was last codified in the first generation, while High Common was last codified [error] generations ago; as a result most contemporary vulgar commons are not mutually intelligible.

- **Old Common** (also: Betterspeak, Serme Madraia) is the original interlanguage taught to all first generation humans by the Mother Machines. An optimal, streamlined language for operating in the stem environment of the Initial Settlement of Heaven. Now a dead language, except among several dozen aristocratic circles and retrograde societies that are certainly not important powers in the invisible markets.
- **Un-Common** (also: Ur-Common, Bilder, Dromspreg) is the old-fashioned cousin dialect of Betterspeak, used by the Dream Lords and their agents. Neither streamlined, nor optimal, it is full of bizarre idioms like “break a leg” and “bolt from the blue” that are patently false. It is often found embedded in timelost personalities when these are excavated.
- **High Common** (also: Canopic, Sermirabilie) is the beautiful 17th generation form of Betterspeak devised by the Dream Canopy for Garden humans. It is the standard language taught among the better classes, its melodious vowels, flexible grammar, and luxurious lexicon offering a special status all its own.

**The Vulgar Common Speeches**

There are 768 officially registered parochial dialects and creoles used by the lesser classes and tribes of the Garden. Since they are neither mutually intelligible nor particularly valuable, most dignified travelers prefer to purchase the services of a tlumac or local interpreter, rather than learn these declined jibber-jabberings.

- **Blue Speeches** (also: Blauenstalk, Azulic) encompass the harsh and uncouth dialects of the fallen folks of the Blue Land and its exiles. Writings in these languages are easily infiltrated by mental corruptions and thus suppressed. Their lexicons are rich resources for those interested in fungi, fermentations, dairy, aquatic vegetables and teleempathetic post-human neurological heresies.
- **Green Speeches** (also: Grenspruks, Meraldi) cover both the peasant and forester oral dialects of the inland Green and the glittering neo-common of Metropolis, the Emerald City, with its countless loan words from Un-Common and Bilder. The latter is known for its broad pop-lit corpus and overdone neotraditional handwriting.
- **Yellow Speeches** (also: Saffranian, Polipoliti, Caravanian) cover a range of urban and nomadic languages in the Yellow Land and beyond in the Yellow Waste and Golden Desert. The Caravanian creoles have a mercantile written tradition, flexible structure, and propensity to cant. The languages of the cities tend to be onomatopoeic, with short words and a preference for understatement. Their refined rhyming lends well to written forms and musical theater.
- **Orange Speeches** (also: Oranjetic, Caddic, Proprish) embrace both the colloquial musical dialects of the little mayfly humans of the contemporary Orange Lands with their paltry written traditions and the ancestral chanted forms used by the administrators and grand necromancers of that fertile land.
- **Red Speeches** (also: Raudalandi, RD Cant) include the rough, dwarfer influenced languages of the lesser vintners of that region, the badly-rhyming vulgar and politically charged traditions of the Red Land District, the grinding, dusty forms of the rustlander seminomadics, and the rich, slippery syllabaries of the long-lived bunker-and-blood clans who first liberated that region from the Ill Gods.
- **Purple Speeches** (also: Purapura, Catastrofict) range from the oral hammer tongues of the Purple Land with their steppe and Blue borrowings to the mildly telepathic acat-demese of the Violet University and the uplifted agents of the Cat Lords.

**The Poor Forms**

Barely speech, it is unclear to humans with good source codes and impeccably machined germ lines how these babblish sounds and gestures could possibly function.

- **Feralspeaks** (also: Steppen, Plateausk, Elfen, etc.) is a neutral and inoffensive term used by enlightened betterfolk of the Circle Sea to refer to the related and unrelated 'languages' spoken by out-Garden sentient communities instead of the more loaded expression 'feralshits'. **The Maintainer Speak[s]**

Long Long Ago, when the machines were first born, a caste of humans was made to keep them glistening and glowing. The Mother Machines bestowed an elegant Newspeak, a streamlined Betterspeak, on these dewerkers. Over aeons maintaining the machinery of the Garden and the golems of the Gods, it has become a family of reasonably human languages, including:

- **Mainttalk** (also: Dwerkfers, Golmtek) is the archaic golem-compatible basic newspeak still implanted in many newly decanted maintainers. Unfortunately, it no longer works on most modern machines.
- **Oltek** (also: 1zvrn, Frygolm) is a collection of heavily feralized Polysolar Dwarfer dialects with a huge golem corpus spoken by wandering relic tribes beyond the Garden's eastern bounds.
- **Volkan** (also: Kemfers, Nonc Golm) refers to the Diesel Dwarfer echolects from the petrochemical generative region of the ungoddess-mountain Vulkana. It has a vast written corpus and the space between characters often carries more meaning than the letters themselves.
- **Winerian** (also: Unfers, Raudakoln) covers the hill and mountain dialects of the Vintner Dwarfer and Red Land industrialists. Derived from the Bunker Builders' jargon and heavily influenced by the Red Speeches, this literalist and linear language has little writing and is much closer to the vulgar commons than other maintainer speaks.

**The Old Codes**

In the beginning was the Word. Before machine or human, semiotes floated in the ooze-soup of potential, fuel for the Maker's Great Speech Act. Fragments of this first language remain.

- **MKR Source** (also: Logik) was/is/will be the language of the word according to the Priests of the Cogflower. It is the magical grammar of the machine-creator of the Given World. Rare humans can grasp a little of this perfect speech and call its utterances incantations and spells. The Builders and the Lords of the Dream Canopy, in their glory, speak MKR Source natively. Heretics say this is the false language of the demiurge, the unconsciousnessmaker, the source of illusion, a darkened mirror of the true Word of the true Creation.
- **Babel** (also: Intermind, Noöphras) is purportedly a human neurology-adapted fork of MKR Source that allows humans to speak directly and perfectly to one another. A native speaker of Babel can understand every form of human speech—if they can also see a speaker’s body language and other subconscious cues. Easily confused with telepathy.
- **Go Language** (also: Golemic, Clicbeep) confusingly refers to the hundreds of forks of machine-speak now spoken by the numerous free and lobotomized golems of the Circle Sea area. Humans can learn to write and read these languages, but the sounds remain beyond them. A properly written command in Go Language may force sentient readers to follow it, whether they want to or not.
- **CAT Astral** (also: Felicithink) is a noöspheric thought-speech extruded into the material setting of the hylosphere. Its formalized logical structure enables empathetic and even telepathic translations of ideas and qualia between separate minds.

Exciting experiments in the Violet University show it has potential for enabling direct mind-to-mind inter-species communication.
