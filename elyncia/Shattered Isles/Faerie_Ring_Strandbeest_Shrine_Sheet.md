# Faerie-Ring Strandbeest-Shrine — Character Sheet

*Support companion sheet for Lady Aki. Leveled item (not a full Hallmark character), Generic Synthesized Creature chassis.*

**Owner:** Lady Aki (Freyja)
**Concept:** Living automaton shrine-wagon — three things synthesized into one: a fantascience shrine ruin, a Mother Mycelium Clutch (fairy ring), and a juvenile strandbeest
**Type:** Generic Synthesized Creature (Living Automaton)
**Level:** 2 (Calibrated item)
**Role:** Mobile Household Lararium (L2), expedition transport, field logistics, Ichi recharging platform, tasked-spirit spawner

---

## Origin

When Lady Aki bonded with Ichi at the old fantascience shrine site, the shrine itself remained — damaged, leaking mana, but still anchored to a ley-line thread. Ichi's Mother Clutch, the fairy ring of mycelium that had birthed the spore-jelly tasked spirit, rooted into the shrine's foundation and began stabilizing it.

Freyja saw an opportunity. A juvenile strandbeest — one of the wind-walking kinetic creatures that wander Elyncia's Gaia-bleed coastlines, descendants of Theo Jansen's sculptures given improbable life — had been grazing near the shrine site. She convinced the Mother Clutch to transplant into the strandbeest's frame, synthesizing shrine, fairy ring, and walking machine into a single mobile platform.

The result: a miniature roadside shrine mounted on articulated wind-walking legs, trailing silk-and-mycelium sails, pulling an armored wagon. The Mother Clutch's fairy ring glows faintly orange at the shrine's base. The strandbeest's legs move with the eerie smooth gait of something that learned to walk from the wind.

Freyja describes her as "a mushroom shrine tachikoma... pulling a wagon."

## Visual Description

**Silhouette:** A compact Japanese-style roadside shrine (hokora) — peaked roof, open front, offering shelf — mounted on a strandbeest chassis of articulated wooden-and-chitin legs. Six to eight legs, depending on how many the juvenile has grown. Roughly cart-height at the shrine platform, with the legs adding lateral width and an unsettling fluidity of motion.

**Shrine body:** Weathered fantascience composite — neither wood nor stone nor metal, but something that reads as all three depending on the light. Orichalcum-inlaid DreamNet glyphs trace the roofline and doorframe, pulsing faintly when the ley-line feed stabilizes. The offering shelf holds a small libation dish (functional — feeding the node activates lararium bonuses).

**Mother Clutch:** A fairy ring of bioluminescent mushrooms rooted at the shrine's base, threading orange-glowing mycelial runners through the strandbeest's joints and the shrine's foundation. The mushrooms pulse slowly, like breathing. When the Clutch spawns a tasked spirit, one mushroom cap opens and releases a floating spore-jelly into the air.

**Strandbeest legs:** Articulated compound limbs of petrified driftwood, chitin segments, and mycelium-silk tendons. They move in a rippling wave-gait — wind-adapted locomotion inherited from the Gaia strandbeest lineage. In still air, the legs fold and the shrine rests like a crab at low tide.

**Sails:** Silk-and-mycelium membranes stretched between lightweight spars extending from the shrine's roof and the strandbeest's upper joints. They catch wind for supplemental locomotion and unfurl bioluminescent patterns at night. When furled, they wrap tight against the shrine body like folded wings.

**Wagon:** A towed armored wagon-bed on simple wheels, connected by a mycelium-reinforced tow-bar. Functional, not decorative — expedition cargo, supplies, and cover. The wagon's armor is chitin-and-composite plate, patched with mycelium scar-tissue in the same style as Ichi's repairs.

**Overall:** Something between a Shinto roadside shrine, a Theo Jansen beach sculpture, and a hermit crab carrying a cathedral on its back. It moves wrong — too smooth, too quiet for something that size. The orange glow from the fairy ring and the mycelium runners gives it the look of something lit from within by banked coals.

## Heritage: Generic Synthesized Creature *(free, no trait slot)*

The Generic Synthesized Creature chassis defines the Strandbeest-Shrine's fundamental nature — how tough she gets, how well-protected she starts, and how hard she hits as she grows. Three heritage-slot effects, matching the pattern underlying all NPC chassis types:

### Slot 1 — Synth Life Progression

Life = 4 + 4×Level through the Small Synth tier (Level 0–3). At Level 4+ (Reinforced Synth tier), the formula accelerates — larger frames, denser structures, more redundant systems.

| Tier | Levels | Life Formula | At L2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Small Synth | 0–3 | 4 + 4×Lvl | **12** |
| Reinforced Synth | 4–7 | Accelerating (~5–6/Lvl) | — |
| Heavy Synth | 8–9 | Further acceleration | — |
| Monster Synth | 10–13 | Major acceleration | — |
| Catastrophe Synth | 14–17 | 300→666 | — |

### Slot 2 — Natural Armor (+3)

Inherent structural protection from the chitin-composite shrine body, mycelium-reinforced joints, and strandbeest frame. This +3 does not come from worn equipment — it represents the creature's physical construction. Cannot be removed. Applies in all modes.

The Living mod can temporarily reduce this armor to fuel self-repair (trade 1 Armor → regain 1d4 Life; rest+feed → regain 1 Armor/week).

### Slot 3 — Synth Damage Progression

Damage die steps upward at tier breaks. Single die through Small Synth; die+flat emerges at Reinforced; compound dice at Heavy and beyond.

| Tier | Levels | Damage Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Small Synth | 0–3 | 1d4 / 1d6 / **1d8** / 1d10 |

| Heavy Synth | 8–9 | 2d8+8 / 1d20+11 |
| Monster+ | 10+ | 1d20+1dX+flat (compound) |

## Mods (3/3 filled — Calibrated, L2)

### Mod 1 — Automaton

> The shrine-creature follows simple instructions but has no independent tactical mind of its own. It walks where directed, stops when told, and defends its cargo with mechanical persistence. In autocombat mode, the strandbeest legs lock into aggressive posture and the shrine's defensive wards activate.

**Passive:** Follows simple verbal or gestural instructions from Aki (or designated operator). Can "walk" the operator, making some endurance challenges trivial (the shrine carries you). No independent decision-making — if instructions conflict or the situation exceeds its parameters, it defaults to holding position and defending.

**Autocombat mode** (activated by command or threat trigger):

| Stat | Formula | At L2 |
|---|---|---|
| STR/AGI/END | +1d4 (temporary boost) | +1d4 |
| Life | 10 × Level | 20 |
| Defense | 7 + Armor + Level | 12 (7+3+2) |
| Attack | +Level | +2 |
| Damage | 1d6 + Armor + Level | 1d6+5 |

Autocombat ends when the threat is neutralized, the shrine is reduced to 0 Life, or Aki issues a stand-down command.

### Mod 2 — Living

> The shrine breathes. The mycelium runs through every joint and strut like a nervous system. When damaged, the fairy ring can divert growth energy from structural reinforcement to tissue repair — trading armor integrity for survival.

**Self-repair:** Reduce Armor bonus by 1 → immediately regain 1d4 Life. Minimum Armor: 0.

**Recovery:** When fed (organic matter, mana-rich nutrients, composted biomass) and allowed to rest, the shrine regains 1 Armor per week as the mycelium regrows structural reinforcement.

**Interaction with Sporemark trait:** If reduced to 0 Life, the Living mod and Sporemark together enable full recovery by burying the fairy ring in living soil for one day. The strandbeest frame may need physical repair, but the core organism reconstitutes.

### Mod 3 — Strandbeest Sails

> Silk-and-mycelium membranes catch the wind. The juvenile strandbeest's Gaia-lineage locomotion, given Elyncian materials.

**Effect:** Functions as Living Sails (vehicle mod equivalent). Adds supplemental speed in favorable wind conditions — not quantified as a mechanical speed boost, but provides advantage in chase/pursuit situations with wind. Reduces weekly supply consumption by a small margin (the sails partially power locomotion, reducing grazing/mana draw).

**Visual:** The sails unfurl bioluminescent patterns at night — the mycelium traces in the silk glow faintly orange, making the shrine visible at distance after dark. This can be suppressed by furling the sails tight, but the default state is a gentle ambient glow. Locals will notice.

## Traits (3/4 slots filled)

### Trait 1 — Sporemark *(Bluelander path)*

> "The mycelium is within you. You hear the all-fungus. Talk to trees and soil and rotting things. If injuries reduce you to 0 life, spending a day buried in living soil restores you to full health."

The Mother Clutch *is* mycelium. This trait defines the shrine's ecological identity — she communicates through fungal networks, reads the health of soil and root systems, and can sense organic decay, growth, and disturbance in her vicinity.

**Recovery mechanic:** If reduced to 0 Life, bury the fairy ring in living soil for one day → full Life restoration. The strandbeest frame and shrine body may need additional physical repair (referee's call), but the organism itself reconstitutes.

**Campaign relevance:** In the Neo-Thracian Nexus, where ley-line networks run through the same substrate as root systems and mycelial mats, Sporemark provides a *physical-layer* diagnostic channel parallel to Ichi's Dreamwalker *noospheric-layer* diagnostics. The shrine reads the soil; Ichi reads the dream. Between them, the expedition has two complementary sensor systems for ley-line triage.

### Trait 2 — Inner Fire

> "+2 trait/item inventory slots, +1 Defeat threshold."

The fairy ring's deep root network and the shrine's fantascience foundation refuse to go down easily. Expanded inventory represents internal compartments in the shrine body, sealed niches under the offering shelf, and the mycelium network's ability to hold small objects in its root-web.

**Inventory effect:** Base 7 slots + 2 from Inner Fire = **9 item slots** (stone-scale).

**Defeat threshold:** +1 to Defeat rolls. The shrine is harder to destroy outright — the mycelium redistributes damage, the strandbeest legs sacrifice themselves to absorb impacts, and the fairy ring retreats deeper into its root ball.

### Trait 3 — Summon Tasked Spirit: Floating Mycelial-Jelly *(Power as Trait)*

> The Mother Clutch retains her original purpose: spawning tasked spirits. She produces floating mycelial-jelly organisms — translucent spore-jellyfish that drift through the air, trailing bioluminescent tendrils.

**Mechanic:** The shrine-creature can spawn a single poly-body homunculus (floating mycelial-jelly tasked spirit). Each spawn costs +bonus "ability damage" — at +4, the Clutch can absorb several spawns before exhaustion, but each one degrades her operational capacity until she heals. The ability damage must heal naturally (rest, feeding, time) before that capacity recovers.

**Spawned spirit:** Uses Tasked Spirit rules (see Elyncia DreamNet documentation). Single-assignment, protocol-compliant, dissolves when the task completes or the binding decays. The jelly's form reflects its assignment — a scouting jelly drifts ahead as an etheric wisp; a guardian jelly coalesces around an object; a messenger jelly pulses with encoded signal.

**Current clutch:** 1 clutch of ashigaru-homunculi available. These are more robust than standard tasked spirits — shaped by the Mother Clutch's original military-logistics purpose (grow ashigaru, send them to work). They can perform simple physical tasks, carry small objects, and serve as expendable scouts or sentries.

### Trait 4 — *(Empty — Freyja undecided)*

Reserved. Candidates discussed in session: Friends (companion management for spawned spirits), From Dust Returning (extreme persistence), Deathless (dormancy-and-recovery cycle), or something that emerges from play.

## Lararium Functions — Mobile Household Shrine (L2)

The Faerie-Ring Strandbeest-Shrine functions as a Level 2 DreamNet node — a walking household lararium. Feeding the node (food, drink, incense, mana-rich offerings at the libation dish) grants the operator one bonus from the standard compact:

**Standard Compact (3 options, choose 1 per feeding):**

1. **Stabilize** — more reliable access to local ley-line mana for the session
2. **Reduce Power mana cost** — −1 mana cost (minimum 1) for Powers used nearby that match the node's affinities
3. **Boost effective casting Level** — +1d6 effective Level for the session, allowing the operator to safely cast Powers whose mana cost would otherwise exceed their character Level without triggering Wild Magic / Corruption exposure rolls

**Node affinities** (derived from Aki's affinities and the shrine's mycelial substrate): `[spore]` `[biomancy]` `[dream]` — other affinities may resonate at referee's discretion.

**Household tier (L1–3):** The noosphere hums but mostly minds its manners. Routine rites, wards that behave. This is intimate, personal, low-draw infrastructure — it stabilizes Aki's immediate circle, not a district. A market-tier node (L4–7) or temple-tier node (L8–12+) would provide significantly greater throughput and range.

**Field recharging for Ichi:** The shrine's ley-line access and mana reserves can serve as a recharging station for Ichi's Powered Symbiote system. Each negative Level recovered by Ichi requires one full recharge cycle at a functioning ley-line node or active lararium. The Strandbeest-Shrine qualifies — the expedition carries its own maintenance infrastructure.

## Notes for Play

**Management overhead:** The Strandbeest-Shrine operates as sentient gear, not a played character. Freyja (or the referee) voices her reactions when relevant — the fairy ring pulsing in response to ley-line signal, the strandbeest legs folding in distress, the sails unfurling in favorable wind. She doesn't take independent actions unless in autocombat or following standing orders.

**Expedition role:** Infrastructure. She carries supplies, tows the wagon, provides a mobile shrine for Aki's casting economy, recharges Ichi, spawns expendable scouts, and reads the soil. In a fight, she's the thing the party protects. In camp, she's the thing that makes camp functional.

**The Mother Clutch question:** The Mother Glade sent the original spore-jelly on a mission: settle, colonize, grow ashigaru, send them home. That mission has been... repurposed. Does the Mother Glade know? Does it care? Is the Strandbeest-Shrine a successful colony or a defection? *Hold this open — parallel to Ichi's personhood question.*

**Visibility:** A walking mushroom shrine with glowing sails, trailing a wagon, is not stealthy. In the Neo-Thracian Nexus — a region that hasn't seen outside contact in 489 years — this thing will attract attention, curiosity, fear, or worship depending on who encounters it. The sails can be furled and the glow suppressed, but the silhouette remains distinctive.

**Supply interaction:** The Living mod, Sporemark trait, and Strandbeest Sails together create a creature that partially feeds itself (wind locomotion, photosynthetic mycelium, organic matter composting). She still consumes supplies, but at a reduced rate compared to a purely mechanical transport of similar capacity.
