# Lady Aki — Concept Art Direction Notes v1

*Not final. Use as design scaffolding for FTLS / SDM+ alpha character creation.*

## Core Art Problem

Aki has a true Spider Folk body, but her traveling/public silhouette is mediated by faerie glamour and shrine etiquette.

The design sheet should show both:
- the chitinous fae body truth,
- and the socially readable wandering miko glamour.

Do **not** collapse these into one fixed costume.

---

# Main Visual Modes

## 1. True Body — Spider Folk Heritage

Use for anatomy studies, side callouts, and partial reveal panels.

Design notes:
- iridescent fae chitin: violet, seafoam, turquoise, moonlit green,
- multiple arms, but elegant and ritual-useful rather than monstrous,
- soft alien face; gentle expression allowed,
- plate-like chitin reads as natural armor,
- subtle web/silk organs or spinneret-tool logic can appear at wrists, hips, or back,
- body should feel fae, mythic, and sacred rather than horror-grotesque.

## 2. Public Glamour — Traveling Miko

Default table-facing Aki.

Design notes:
- red/white miko work outfit,
- silksteel ritual robe layers,
- purple or dark outer travel layer optional,
- classic wide-brimmed **ichimegasa** with **uchikatsugi-style veil**,
- veil softens identity and hides chitinous silhouette,
- extra arms may be hidden, softened, or visually interpreted as sleeve-folds / shadow / ribbon until revealed,
- shrine bells, ofuda, sash tools, spinneret kit, and small charms at belt.

## 3. Threshold Reveal — Glamour Thins

Use for dynamic action thumbnails.

Design notes:
- veil lifts or tears aside,
- chitin catches light,
- extra arms anchor silk lines or hold ofuda,
- bells and ward papers multiply,
- silk geometry defines the room,
- Aki remains a controller / ritualist, not a front-line bruiser.

# Hat Direction

Prefer the **classic wide-brimmed traveling hat** over the flatter hat in some reference images.

Use:
- ichimegasa silhouette,
- broad straw/bamboo/reed brim,
- hanging veil,
- travel-priestess / aruki-miko energy,
- not a tall court ritual hat.

---

# Ichi Direction

Ichi should **not** have spider legs.

Use Ichi's character file:
- compact ashigaru infantry armor,
- medium humanoid frame,
- slightly stocky practical sergeant silhouette,
- chitin kikko-pattern cuirass,
- mycelium-silk backing,
- glowing orange daemon-spore eye-orbs,
- damaged but repaired armor with orange scar-light,
- old soldier / guardian / living powered armor.

When worn:
- Ichi wraps Aki as armor.
- glowing eye-spots migrate upward above Aki's brow like a guardian crest.
- Ichi does not become a spider-legged creature.

When autonomous:
- Ichi reads as a compact humanoid armor-person, not a spider mech.

---

# Strandbeest-Shrine Direction

The **shrine** is the one with articulated legs.

Use:
- compact roadside shrine / hokora,
- strandbeest kinetic leg logic,
- six to eight walking limbs,
- silk-and-mycelium sails,
- chitin/composite shrine body,
- offering shelf and libation dish,
- orange-glowing Mother Spore fairy ring,
- armored wagon-bed,
- Ephyra / spore-jelly outputs.

It may feel like:
- Shinto roadside shrine,
- Theo Jansen strandbeest,
- hermit crab cathedral,
- mushroom shrine tachikoma.

---

# Prismatic Elf Ranger Friend

For group sheets:
- include the Prismatic Elf ranger / kaiju-stalker as Aki's expedition peer,
- large grimalkin-lynx mount,
- clear silhouette separation:
  - Ranger = wilderness / hunt / kaiju-scale threat reading.
  - Aki = shrine / threshold / veil / support infrastructure.
  - Shrine = logistics / camp / lararium.
  - Ichi = guardian powered armor.

Do not let the ranger and Aki merge visually.

---

# Poster-Sized Landscape Design Sheet Brief

Create a **landscape-oriented poster concept sheet**, readable like video game design concept art, not a final polished illustration.

Suggested layout:
1. Center-left: Aki in public glamour, full body, veiled ichimegasa, red/white robe.
2. Center-right: Aki with glamour thinning, chitin and extra arms partially visible.
3. Rear/right: Ichi as compact humanoid ashigaru powered armor, no spider legs.
4. Rear/left or background: Faerie-Ring Strandbeest-Shrine with articulated strandbeest legs.
5. Lower strip: detail callouts — hat, veil, spinneret kit, safety netsuke, bell-tikker, silksteel robe texture, ofuda, chitin plates.
6. Small inset: Prismatic Elf ranger and grimalkin-lynx scale reference near shrine.
7. Margin notes: rough concept labels, arrows, non-final sketch annotations.

Negative direction:
- no spider legs on Ichi,
- no tall ritual hat,
- no hypersexual framing,
- no final-production polish,
- no generic witch hat,
- no modern sci-fi armor replacing shrine/ashigaru language.


# Talk Story Notes — Concept Art Package v1

## What Changed

The character creation package now treats Aki's concept art as a three-register design:

1. True Spider Folk body.
2. Public glamour / traveling miko presentation.
3. Threshold reveal / danger geometry.

The concept art of Aki remains explicitly useful, but not final. It contributes scale, party ecology, and the idea that Aki's faerie glamour hides or softens her chitinous form.

## The Big Fix

Ichi does not have the spider-legged silhouette.

Ichi remains a compact humanoid ashigaru powered armor companion: chitin plates, mycelium-silk backing, orange daemon-spore eyes, damaged-sergeant practicality.

The mobile shrine carries the articulated strandbeest legs.

## Why This Matters

This prevents three visual systems from collapsing into one:

- Aki's Spider Folk body = heritage truth.
- Ichi = guardian powered armor / personhood question.
- Strandbeest-Shrine = expedition infrastructure / mobile lararium.

The concept package now gives artists a clean separation of silhouettes.

## Table Read

When Aki walks into a settlement:
- locals first see veiled miko traveler,
- careful observers notice too many sleeve-folds,
- hostile pressure makes chitin and extra hands appear,
- Ichi waits as armor/person,
- the shrine clicks behind her like a household that learned to walk.
