# Sidebar — Alpha Playtest Company Structure and Multi-PC Play v3

## Purpose

This sidebar displays the **full player-side company** for the FTLS/SDM+ alpha playtest in Neo-Thracia. It separates table authority, player-daemon control, primary characters, Hallmark companions, sentient infrastructure, tasked spirits, crew assets, and cargo buckets.

The campaign uses an **advanced multi-PC setup**: each player-daemon may run a Primary Character plus one or more sidekick/Hallmark assets, while Telarus referees the world, opposition, neutral parties, faction pressure, and most company-adjacent mook NPCs.

---

## Telarus

**Role:** Referee, Game Designer, Wild Mage

Telarus controls:
- mook NPCs attached to the company or encountered in play,
- factions,
- ruins,
- environments,
- logistics pressure,
- politics,
- DreamNet pressures,
- hostile and neutral actors,
- ordinary reactions from Neo-Thracian locals, outsiders, cults, patrols, and institutions.

Telarus does **not** use the player-side company to solve problems for the players. Referee-controlled allies create pressure, complications, opportunities, and continuity; player-daemons make the expedition’s load-bearing decisions.

## Company Infrastructure

The company currently operates under **Adventurer’s Guild cover**, sponsored by the Lindwyrm at **Guild Rank 1**. The Guild cover provides basic legitimacy and prismatic ID keys, but it also creates trails: papers, recognitions, expectations, and outsider signals.

Shared company assets include:
- the small ship and working ship stores,
- ship cargo space for company equipment,
- Aki’s mobile shrine support platform,
- Ichi’s repair/recharge needs,
- Ephyra & Fungus Gumi tasking capacity,
- sidekick/Hallmark logistics,
- pooled expedition gear,
- Lindwyrm credit instruments and paper trails,
- Adventurer’s Guild credentials and rank-1 cover.

Neo-Thracia has been isolated for roughly 500 years. Guild credentials may open doors with Nexus-aware outsiders, but may also mark the company as suspicious, foreign, politically useful, or dangerous to local cultures that evolved without recent Noosphere contact.

## Control Doctrine

Use the smallest control scope that keeps play moving.

- **Primary character spotlight:** the relevant player-daemon controls their PC.
- **Hallmark spotlight:** the owning player-daemon controls the companion, mount, pet, powered symbiote, or sidekick when it performs player-side action.
- **Infrastructure spotlight:** the owning player-daemon controls sentient infrastructure when commanded, spotlighted, or when its procedures matter.
- **Company-scale logistics:** use the shared company sheet, with the gorgonne captain / ship player-daemon tracking ship cargo and Telarus applying pressure.
- **Mook NPCs and neutral allies:** Telarus usually controls them unless a rule, scene, or explicit handoff says otherwise.
- **Factions and environments:** Telarus controls them as normal.

---

## Design Goal

Keep Aki’s support powerful but bounded, and keep the whole company legible at the table:

- tools create options,
- rites take time,
- Ephyra need clear tasks,
- shrine work consumes offerings,
- silk systems need anchors,
- Ichi needs repair and recharge support,
- mounts and pets need food, space, and scene logic,
- crew assets need pay, morale, and command clarity,
- ship cargo needs a shared ledger,
- Guild credentials create paper trails,
- Neo-Thracian locals may react unpredictably to Nexus-visible authority.

The intended play pattern: **the company can do more than a normal small party, but every extra capability creates a footprint, upkeep cost, vulnerability, or social signal.**
