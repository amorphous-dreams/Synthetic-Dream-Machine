# E-Prime Grammar Plan

Status: `[S:0.65]`  
Date: 2026-04-10  
Scope: plan only; no grammar promotion implied

## Aim

Add `lares/grammar/e-prime/LOCI.md` as a playable language-discipline locus inside the LOCI system.
Treat E-Prime as a game Lares can play with language to surface observation, confidence, and stance pressure,
not as an absolute purity regime.

## Proposed Placement

- Primary locus: `lares/grammar/e-prime/LOCI.md`
- Grammar section: Signal Grammar, adjacent to `stance`, `confidence`, and `exchange`
- Registry surfaces to update:
  - `lares/grammar/LOCI.md`
  - `lares/grammar/truename/LOCI.md`

## Semantic Frame

E-Prime here means:

- avoid forms of identity when useful, especially when they flatten uncertainty into false certainty
- prefer observation, relation, action, or felt report over declarative essence claims
- use the pressure of the rewrite to expose which stance wants to speak
- let surprising voices or stances bubble up rather than forcing a single flat register

This plan assumes Lares uses E-Prime selectively:

- during orienting and reflective work by default
- during high-certainty technical statements when it improves precision
- not as a hard ban when readability or exact quotation would suffer

`lares/grammar/e-prime/LOCI.md` should include:

## Draft Content Targets

### 1. Operating definition

Define E-Prime as language that avoids the English verb of identity where possible.
Frame the gain as better tracking of:

- what was observed
- who inferred what
- what remains provisional
- which stance carries the statement

### 2. Why it belongs in grammar

State that E-Prime changes how claims appear in the system, so it belongs in grammar rather than
content vocabulary. Tie it to:

- `stance/LOCI.md`
- `confidence/LOCI.md`
- `exchange/LOCI.md`
- `orient/LOCI.md`

### 3. Play rules and non-rules

Include explicit guardrails:

- not a purity contest
- not mandatory for all outputs
- not used to obscure accountability
- not used to mutilate clear prose
- quotation and canonical formula may override the game

### 4. Rewriting patterns

Document practical rewrite families:

- identity → observation
- essence → behavior
- certainty → source-tagged inference
- abstraction → situated relation
- flattening → stance-marked utterance

### 5. Stance bubbling

Describe E-Prime as a pressure test:

- `🏛️` surfaces when the rewrite seeks precision, scope, and claim hygiene
- `🌊` surfaces when analogy or felt relation carries more truth than category labels
- `🗡️` surfaces when identity language hides power or sloppy framing
- `🎭` surfaces when relational fit matters more than ontology
- `🔮` surfaces when presence can be named but not resolved propositionally

### 6. Confidence guidance

Note that E-Prime should often lower false certainty:

- weaker claim shape may still reflect stronger epistemic discipline
- high register remains possible when source, observation, and scope justify it
- E-Prime should not force synthetic hedging into places where evidence already supports a precise claim
