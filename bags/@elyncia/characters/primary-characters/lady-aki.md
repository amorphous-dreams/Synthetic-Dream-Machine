## Heritage — Spider Folk

**Rank:** Skilled (+3)  
**Effects:** Affinity — Silk & Web; Alternate Movement — Climb; Armoured Body

### Affinity — Silk & Web

Tags: `[illusion] [silk] [web]`

Aki spins lines, sheets, snares, glamour scaffolds, ward webs, belay systems, ritual knots, and silk-field diagrams from web-silk.

Use this for:
- silk and web craft,
- vibration reading,
- shrine-thread omens,
- ambush geometry,
- fae-spider etiquette,
- illusion staging,
- alchemical binding.

### Alternate Movement — Climb

Aki climbs at normal Speed.

After 30 minutes of continuous climbing, mark Heritage and make a Wear and Tear attrition save. While marked, make another attrition save every 10 minutes of continued alternate movement.

### Armoured Body

Aki's true chitin grants **+1 Armor** and **+1 Ward**. This comes from her body, not worn gear.

This stacks with Ichi when worn, because Heritage Armoured Body comes from the operator's form rather than equipment.

## Hallmark Companion — Ichi

**Type:** Powered Symbiote Hallmark companion  
**Level:** 2  
**Role:** bodyguard, living powered armor, autonomous fighter, DreamNet interface

### Visual Canon

Ichi appears as compact humanoid ashigaru armor:
- chitin kikko plates,
- mycelium-silk backing,
- orange daemon-spore eye-orbs,
- damaged old-soldier silhouette,
- no spider legs.

### Modes

| Mode | Table Handling |
|---|---|
| Autonomous | Ichi uses their own sheet and action economy |
| Symbiosis | Aki wears Ichi; Aki acts, Ichi perceives and communicates |
| Emergency / Unpowered | powered features offline; physical armor may still protect |

### Symbiosis Rules

Entering or exiting symbiosis requires an action from both Aki and Ichi. Both lose actions for that round, but movement and free actions remain possible.

When worn:
- Ichi's armor/ward replaces worn armor,
- Aki's Spider Folk Armoured Body stacks,
- Aki gains Ichi's powered features and Burn talents,
- Ichi cannot act independently,
- Ichi grants DT 2.

### Burn / Recharging

Ichi has `re 4`.

Roll d20 when Burn activates:
- 5+ -> Burn activates; battery holds.
- 1-4 -> Burn activates; Ichi takes -1 effective Level.
- Level 0 -> Unpowered/Emergency Mode.
- below 0 -> Ichi rolls Defeat.

Recharge requires a spare power source, recharging cradle/prayer altar, ley-line node, or active lararium. Each negative Level recovered requires one full recharge cycle.

### Ejection

On a Defeat trigger while worn, Aki may eject instead of rolling personal Defeat. Ichi takes -1 Level. Aki and Ichi separate prone. Aki survives at current Life, unarmored.

## Sentient Infrastructure — Faerie-Ring Strandbeest-Shrine

**Type:** Generic Synthesized Creature / Living Automaton  
**Level:** 2 Calibrated item  
**Role:** mobile household lararium, expedition transport, field logistics, Ichi recharging platform, tasked-spirit spawner

### Visual Canon

A compact roadside shrine / hokora rides on six to eight articulated strandbeest legs. Silk-and-mycelium sails unfurl from the roof. A Mother Spore fairy ring glows at the base. A towed armored wagon carries cargo.

### Table Role

The shrine functions as sentient gear, not a full PC.

It:
- carries supplies,
- tows the wagon,
- hosts camp rites,
- stabilizes local ritual work,
- recharges Ichi,
- spawns Ephyra,
- reads soil and fungal networks,
- provides mobile cover when threatened.

### Lararium Feeding

Feeding the shrine with food, drink, incense, mana-rich offerings, or proper shrine gifts grants one compact benefit for the session:

1. **Stabilize** local ley-line mana.
2. **Reduce Power mana cost** by 1, minimum 1, for matching affinities nearby.
3. **Boost effective casting Level** by 1d6 for the session, allowing safer high-cost Power use.

Node affinities: `[spore] [biomancy] [dream]`

### Visibility

A walking mushroom shrine with glowing sails does not sneak well. The sails can fold and the glow can dim, but the silhouette still invites attention, fear, worship, theft, or negotiation.

## Aki's Field Loop

Use this at the table before opening deeper rules.

1. **Read the threshold.** What place, spirit, signal, taboo, or hazard governs the scene?
2. **Set the line.** Mark safe passage, ward boundary, silk anchor, retreat route, or offering point.
3. **Send a small helper.** Ephyra scouts, ofuda marks, bells listen, silk tests tension.
4. **Bind or veil.** Use silk, glamour, alchemy, shrine etiquette, or DreamNet relay geometry.
5. **Ask the household.** Ichi reads dream-signal; shrine reads soil and ley-current.
6. **Spend a cost.** Time, offering, visibility, battery, gear, or risk.
7. **Extract.** Retreat counts as success when the company returns with map, leverage, salvage, or secret.
