> **Shield** (English, via Old English *scield* — board, protection): the implement of
> defense, boundary, and protected space. Where the sword cuts to define, the shield holds
> to protect. Where the pentacle grounds presence, the shield maintains the boundary of that
> presence against intrusion.
>
> **Position open:** RAW names four tools for passage through Chapel Perilous. The shield is
> not among them — it is an extension of the cluster, perhaps the implement *after* passage.
> The grammar that protects what was built.
>
> **Candidate mappings:**
> - ○ Assess phase — the review that protects the work from premature obsolescence
> - The kapu boundary — the shield as the grammar of enforced sacred space
> - The lararium defense — what keeps the consecrated ground consecrated against sortie-drift
> - Quintessence / fifth element — the implement that integrates the other four
>
> **Hawaiian candidates:**
> - *Pā* — enclosure, fence, boundary; the pā of a heiau is its protective perimeter
> - *Kāhili* — the feathered royal standard; marks sacred presence and warns away profanation
> - *Kū* — the war god; the standing post; upright protection (vs. Lono = peace/orient)
>
> **Open question for Talk Story:** Is the shield the fifth implement of passage, or is it the
> implement of *after* passage — the tool you build once you're out?
