Beta playtest material. Here there be dragons.

# New Traits

## Commander

**Skilled/Expert/Master**: +3/+6/+9 to lore, tactics, and rally tests.

When using Formations & Mass Combat, a Commander runs a Formation (self + attached Units/vehicles). Draw a Formation Inventory box on the sheet and list attached Units (and ship stations). Draw a small Command Dice (d6) box beside it.

**Skilled**: attach up to 3 Units within 1 zone; start combat with 1 Command Die.
**Expert**: attach up to 5 Units within 2 zones; start with 2 Command Dice.
**Master**: attach up to 7 Units within 3 zones; start with 3 Command Dice.

*Who rolls*: When an attached Unit acts, the Commander makes the single attack roll using that Unit’s attack type and may spend a Command Die (d6) to add to the roll; the Unit rolls its own damage. The referee determines which Unit members are actually engaged (in reach / line-of-effect) and therefore contribute damage. If no Commander is present, a random Unit member rolls instead.

## Daemon (Interplanar Being)

Your consciousness roams the noosphere. You may choose any number of favorites (usually PCs). When you dedicate time to observing, advising, haunting, or otherwise being present with a favorite, gain 1 Hero Die; gain another for every few hours spent communing/commuting with them. You may hold Hero Dice up to the Level of your highest-level favorite PC.

Spend Hero Dice using the standard rules. The referee may award you Experience; you bank it and invest it into your favorites (PCs, sidekicks, hallmarks) and their upgrades. Other daemons must have permission (from you or the table’s established authority) to spend their banked XP or Hero Dice on your favorites.

## God (Immortal)

An immortal divine program / living principle in the Vastlands sense: fragmented, transactional, and commonly expressed through infrastructure, rites, relics, and agents rather than a single body. Choose a Name and Domain (one short phrase: Roads, Ash, Contracts, Mercy, Rot, Signal, Storm, etc.). An organization may exist (cult, bureaucracy, guild, temple-net, pilgrimage route, saint-chain)… or only scraps remain, a small god running on inertia, rumor, and broken shrines.

Once per session, when mortals invoke this god by rite, oath, sacrifice, or service, mark this trait to deliver a Blessing: grant advantage on one roll, ignore one burden for a day, or ask the referee one clear yes/no question about a place where icons, worship, or jurisdiction hold. The trait cannot be marked if already marked. Unmark it only when the god is meaningfully fed: a real sacrifice, a risk taken in its name, or a complication accepted (referee adjudicates), or after a week of quiet within the Domain.

> Favorites, Experience custody, and Hero Dice belong to Daemon (Interplanar Being). A god may also have Daemon, but it’s rare.

## God Touched

**Skilled**: You gain +1 Ability point, and your daemon can hold \+1 max HD. You are also Lucky: Mark this trait to reroll one test. You must accept the second result.  You may not mark this trait if it is already marked. Unmark this trait by one of the following methods: Rest for one watch (4 hours), or Rest for 10 minutes and spend 1 Life, or Rest for an action and take 1 point of Ability damage.

**Expert**: +2 Ability points, \+2 max HD, Lucky.

**Master**: +3 Ability points, \+3 max HD, Lucky.

## Heritage

Heritage is a birthright, lineage, construction, or mythic origin that shapes how you exist in the world. It grants fictional positioning, esoteric permissions, and a small package of concrete effects.

Choose a heritage descriptor (e.g. Goblin Folk, Awakened Golem, Deathless Revenant, Star-Marked Human). This descriptor defines what kinds of lore, rituals, environments, and effects naturally apply to you.

**Skilled**: +3 to heritage-related lore and research. Choose three Heritage effects.

**Expert**: +6 to heritage-related lore. Choose four Heritage effects.

**Master**: +9 to heritage-related lore. Choose five Heritage effects.

Heritage effects are selected from the OSR Heritage lists (Chapter 05). Effects may grant resistances, needs (or lack thereof), senses, movement modes, immunities, taboos, upkeep requirements, or other non-class advantages and obligations.

Notes:

- Heritage is free at character creation and occupies 1 Trait slot.
- Heritage effects may impose costs, maintenance, or vulnerabilities; these are part of the bargain.
- When in doubt, Heritage defines what is normal for you and what requires no roll.

## Noospheric Agent (Minor-Daemon)

A bound noospheric utility-spirit attached to an Anchor (*person/item/vehicle/ward*) with a Directive (*translate, watch, route, diagnose, map dream-noise, maintain wards, etc.*). While anchored and within its Directive, grants +3 to relevant tests and routine assistance (referee sets limits).

**Overclock (1/watch)**: Choose one effect, then pay one cost.
*Effect*: (a) Push Output — accomplish a bigger-than-usual directive action; (b) Take the Wheel — agent executes the directive action for the Anchor; (c) Shield — reduce one noospheric/magitech complication or gain advantage on one save vs noospheric effects this watch.
*Cost*: Anchor takes 1 stress burden (fitting), or the agent burns out (mark this Trait, it is inert until unmarked - repaired or re-captured).

## Spirit

Your *Ha* (body) is out of phase and insubstantial. Your *Ka* (soul) and *Ba* (psyche) remain fully present. You exist in the same reality as everyone else, but without a stable physical body. While unmanifested, you are incorporeal: you gain +6 Defense vs. physical attacks, may pass through mundane matter, and still suffer Life damage normally. Named, warded, enchanted, or noospheric effects can always affect you.

To act physically, you must manifest your Ha, spending 1 Life for one round or 3 Life for one watch (4 hours). While manifested, you use physical Abilities, may carry Strength stones in gear, and interact normally. When manifestation ends, your body slips out of phase again.

Passing through significant barriers (fortifications, sanctified ground, active machinery) requires a d20 + Thought test vs. Area Level + 10; failure repels or delays you.

**Skilled**: Sense spirits and noospheric disturbances within Thought × 10 yards.

**Expert**: Manifest for one watch for 1 Life, carry up to Strength +3 stones while manifested.

**Master**: Gain Affinity – Chaos • Dream • Shadow (as a Heritage-style effect).

# New Burdens

## Arcane (*Ka-Ba*) Misalignment

Your *Ka* and *Ba* are misaligned with the local noosphere. While affected, the local Area Level penalizes arcane interaction: increase Power and Ritual costs by +Area Level, and increase Hazard or Corruption severity or apply –Area Level to relevant saves or effect rolls.

Affects magitech, fantascience, rituals, daemon bargains, and noospheric effects. Mundane actions are unaffected. This burden may be taken multiple times. Each instance applies the normal –1 to all rolls, but does not further increase Area Level–based penalties. 

## Void Madness

Constructs, revenant undead, uplifted vomes, and other Deathless beings require regular upkeep. Without it, their Ka–Ba destabilizes.

Upkeep: Each week without maintenance, make an Aura or Endurance save. On a failure, take 1 Aura or Endurance damage; if damage cannot be taken, gain Wear & Tear (one instance, one burden slot). Repeat weekly until upkeep is performed.

Void Madness: If a failed upkeep check causes Wear & Tear burdens to exceed the character’s level, they gain Void Madness, which occupies 1 additional burden slot (-1 to all rolls).

While in Void Madness they always act last in initiative and behave erratically, and upkeep checks are suspended. When damaged, the character’s total Burden penalty is inverted and applied as a bonus to attack and damage against the perceived source of that damage, until it leaves sensory range.

Recovery: Remove Void Madness through specialist repair or rites (e.g. artificers, necrotechs, nethermancers). Once removed, upkeep checks resume and Wear & Tear may be cleared per standard rules.
