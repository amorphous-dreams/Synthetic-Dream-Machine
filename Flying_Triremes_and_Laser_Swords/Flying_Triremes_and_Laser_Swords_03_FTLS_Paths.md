### Commander Path

0. **Commander.** **Skilled/Expert/Master**: +3/+6/+9 to lore, tactics, and rally tests.

When using Formations & Mass Combat, a Commander runs a Formation (self + attached Units/vehicles). Draw a Formation Inventory box on the sheet and list attached Units (and ship stations). Draw a small Command Dice (d6) box beside it.

**Skilled**: attach up to 3 Units within 1 zone; start combat with 1 Command Die.
**Expert**: attach up to 5 Units within 2 zones; start with 2 Command Dice.
**Master**: attach up to 7 Units within 3 zones; start with 3 Command Dice.

*Who rolls*: When an attached Unit acts, the Commander makes the single attack roll using that Unit’s attack type and may spend a Command Die (d6) to add to the roll; the Unit rolls its own damage. The referee determines which Unit members are actually engaged (in reach / line-of-effect) and therefore contribute damage. If no Commander is present, a random Unit member rolls instead.

## Wild Mage

\> “We occur at random among your children, the laughter between lightning strikes.” — Telarus, Keeper of the Sacred Chao, Guru of Kilat Pencak Silat, Sorcerer of the Amorphous Dreams Cabal

Wild Mages are born where ley‑lines knot and possibility frays: children who fingerprint with sparks, teenagers who whistle up viridian dust‑devils, caravan guards who juggle fire until the flames start juggling back. Scholars insist they are statistical outliers; priests call them avatars of entropy; most folk just… call them when trouble’s coming. A Wild Mage can turn a skirmish with raiders into a light‑show—or a quiet campfire into a violet crater. Living batteries of pure chance, they are greeted with equal parts awe, hope, and the nervous counting of fire‑bucket charms.

### Wild Mage Path

0. **Wild Mage.** You are attuned to unstable forces. Choose Magitech or Fantascience proficiency; when you reach Master, you are proficient in both. When you succeed on a Dangerous Magic exposure test, you may recover 1 point of Ability damage or 1 Life. 
   - You may mark this trait to push probability, power, or action beyond safe limits. You may not mark this trait if it is already marked.
   - Mark to gain advantage on one attack or ability test (Expert: also saving throws; Master: also Corruption / Wild-Magic exposure tests).
   - If you cast a spell or activate a power while this trait is marked, it automatically triggers a Dangerous Magic exposure test.
   - Unmark this trait by one of the following methods: Rest for one watch (4 hours), or Rest for 10 minutes and spend 1 Life, or Rest for an action and take 1 point of Ability damage.

### Spirit Path

1. **Spirit.** Your *Ha* (body) is out of phase and insubstantial. Your *Ka* (soul) and *Ba* (psyche) remain fully present, and you exist in the same reality as everyone else. While unmanifested, you are incorporeal: you gain +6 Defense vs. physical attacks, may pass through mundane matter, and still suffer Life damage normally. Named, warded, enchanted, or noospheric effects can always affect you.

To act physically, you must manifest your Ha, spending 1 Life for one round or 3 Life for one watch (4 hours). While manifested, you use physical Abilities, may carry Strength stones in gear, and interact normally. Passing through significant barriers (fortifications, sanctified ground, active machinery) requires a d20 + Thought test vs. Area Level + 10; failure repels or delays you. When manifestation ends, your body slips out of phase again.

**Skilled**: Sense spirits and noospheric disturbances within Thought × 10 yards.

**Expert**: Manifest for one watch for 1 Life, carry up to Strength +3 stones while manifested.

**Master**: Gain Affinity – Chaos • Dream • Shadow (as a Heritage-style effect).
