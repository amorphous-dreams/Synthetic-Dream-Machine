# Formations & Mass Combat

*A modular add‑on that sits cleanly on Vastlands’ core cadence: one attack roll (d20 \+ ability \+ skill vs Defense), then damage.*

## **Units**

**When many act as one.** A Unit is a clutch of 2–8 individuals that resolves a single attack together using the game’s four attack types—**Melee (Strength)**, **Ranged (Agility)**, **Magitech/Oldtech (Thought)**, **Fantascience/Sorcery (Charisma)**.

**Declare the Unit’s attack type** for this turn, then make **one Command attack roll** against the target’s Defense; on a hit, the Unit rolls damage from its members’ gear/traits. No Commander nearby? A random Unit member rolls.

**Who actually hits.** Only members with a line of attack contribute damage (adjacent for melee; in range/line‑of‑sight for ranged/tech/powers). “Ready Attack” resolves before an enemy enters range, which pairs naturally with reachy/overwatch tactics.

### **Damage Modes**

After a hit, choose one mode and roll contributing members’ damage dice (use each member’s weapon or effect die, plus any normal bonuses your table allows).

* **Focused.** Sum all contributing dice into one damage source against a single target.  
* **Spread.** Roll each contributor’s damage as its own source and assign each to eligible targets in the scrum.

Damage is still “abstract” and reduces life unless rules say otherwise. Mixed‑kit Units are fine: each member rolls their own weapon’s die. Powers/oldtech/fantascience use the same chassis—pick that attack type, roll once to hit, then treat each contributor’s effect die as a damage die.

### **Defenses & Gear Effects — Order of Operations**

For **every single damage source**, apply:

1. **Negate‑one** effects (e.g., Sunder shields sacrificed to cancel one attack entirely).  
2. **Halving** effects (terrain, cover, armor features like Bulletproof/Mirror, powers that say “half damage”).  
3. **Damage Threshold / Flare** (see Commander & Formations below).

### **Damage Threshold (DT)**

**DT is a protection floor.** After a hit, once you’ve handled negate-one and halving as above, compare each **single damage source** to DT: if it’s **below DT**, it **fails to penetrate** and is **ignored**; if it **meets or exceeds DT**, apply damage normally.

**Who has DT.**

* **Attached Units:** While attached to a Commander, a Unit’s DT equals the **lowest Armor \+ Shield** among its members—the formation’s weak point.  
* **Unattached Units & heroes:** Default **DT 0**, **unless** gear, traits, terrain, or tech explicitly grant **DT** or **Flare**. Certain mounts (e.g., **mecha**) can provide DT/Flare to individuals or to the Unit they’re part of.

## **Commander (Trait) & Formations**

A character with **Commander** leads a **Formation**: themselves plus attached Units (and ships/vehicles) tracked in a **Formation Inventory** box on the character sheet. Use tokens or pips to visibly “mark” units for tempo and reactions. Draw a **Command dice** box nearby.

* **Skilled (+3):** up to **3 Units** within **1 zone**, start combat with **1 Combat die**  
* **Expert (+6):** up to **5 Units** within **2 zones**, start combat with **2 Combat die**  
* **Master (+9):** up to **7 Units** within **3 zones**, start combat with **3 Combat die**

“Zones” use the Vastlands Guidebook’s movement‑and‑range framework (here/nearby/short/over there/long/off‑stage); indoor and vehicular scenes naturally compress/expand those bands.

### **Who rolls**

For each attached Unit that acts this round, the **Commander makes the single attack roll** using that Unit’s chosen attack type; the **Unit rolls its own damage dice**.

### **Attached Units: Brace, Marks, and Command Dice**

* **Brace.** An attached Unit may **Brace** against a single incoming damage source: **mark** the Unit and **halve** that source; the Unit may **forfeit** its next normal activation to unmark. (This echoes “take cover” and other defensive choices that reduce incoming pressure.)  
* **Marking & unmarking.** When a Unit Braces or uses a Commander‑granted reaction, **mark** it. On the Commander’s turn, any marked Unit that **skips its activation** becomes **unmarked**—tempo debt paid. A marked Unit **can’t react again** until it unmarks; **one reaction per Unit per opposing action**.  
* **Command Dice.** Each Commander has a command‑dice pool (**max \= level**). At the start of **each side’s turn**, each Commander on that side gains **\+1 die** (to max).

**Command reaction windows** (each reaction costs **1 die**; a Unit that reacts is **marked**):

* **Movement begins (enemy): Countermove / Receive Charge.** One attached Unit may move and/or make one reaction attack if conditions allow (e.g., “Ready Attack” or clear line). Resolves **before contact** when triggered by readiness, otherwise **after movement**.  
* **Attack declared (enemy): Bodyguard / Screen.** Redirect one single damage source targeting a priority piece (commander, artillery) to an adjacent screening Unit; that screen Unit is considered **Braced**.  
* **Damage rolled (before mitigation):**  
  * **Volley Fire.** If two or more attached Units have hits on the same target this round, **sum those instances into one source**—excellent versus **Hull/Unit DT** (still one attack for Sunder/Flare).  
  * **Guard.** One attached Unit **Braces without being marked** or forfeiting its next action this time (the halving still occurs before DT/Flare).

**Contested Commander rolls.** If both sides trigger Unit reactions in the same window, each rolls **d20 \+ relevant ability \+ Commander skill**; higher resolves first (tie → higher rank; still tied → reroll).

## Notes

### Initiative & Multiple Formations

Use side‑based initiative as written; multiple Formations may act on the same side. Command Dice create move/countermove inside that broad cadence.

### Damage (Focused vs Spread vs DT) — At a Glance

**Designer cue.** Higher DT and Flare push play toward **Focused** hits and command-driven **Volley**; lower DT accelerates attrition and makes **Spread** shine.

* **DT vs Spread.** Many small sources get shaved post‑halving and below‑DT; great for harassing low‑protection lines and controlling space.  
* **DT vs Focused.** One big source beats DT once; vulnerable to Sunder and Flare’s one‑attack limit.

**Damage types & specials (optional tags).**  
 If you use damage tags (piercing, beam, concussive, etc.), apply their riders in the same sequence as above **before** comparing them to DT. Examples: **Mirror** vs **beam** might halve first, then check DT; undead that **halve slashing** do so before DT.

 Critical riders and post-hit effects (frag shrapnel, burning ticks) are **new sources**—run the sequence for each. “Irresistible” ongoing damage folds into the round it’s dealt; later ticks are separate sources.

**Benchmarks (calibrate to your gear lists).**

* **Militia / skirmishers:** DT **2–3** (leather \+ buckler)  
* **Line infantry / hoplons:** DT **3–4** (mail \+ shield)  
* **Elite / heavy:** DT **4–5** (plate \+ large shield)  
* **Pavise wall:** DT **5–6**, **Flare 8–10** (once/round)  
* **Mecha / light vehicles:** **Hull DT 5–7**, **Flare 10–12**  
* **Hydrofoil trireme / medium vehicle:** **Hull DT 6–9**, **Flare 10–12**  
* **Gunships / frigates:** **Hull DT 8–10**, **Flare 12–16**  
* **Capital hull / fortress:** **Hull DT 12+**, **Flare 18+**

### Ships & Vehicles — Same Chassis, Bigger Toys

Treat vehicles from mounts to gunships using the same procedure:

1. Pick attack type (rams/mecha fists \= Melee; guns \= Ranged; Oldtech; Fantascience).  
2. One attack roll per weapon system or battery as fiction fits.  
3. Damage: roll the weapon’s dice. Choose Focused (sum into one source) or Spread (assign by turret/arc/crew).  
4. Mitigation: sacrificial plates/Sunder‑analogues, halving (screens/evasion), then **Hull DT / Flare** (many hulls and pavises use “dissipator” tech that caps one attack per round).

**Stations as Formations.** Large vehicles list stations—Gunnery, Helm, Damage Control, Boarding Party—each staffed by Unit(s) in a Formation’s Inventory; undermanned stations impose disadvantage/penalties, and boarding is zone‑to‑zone movement using the normal zone rules.

### Mecha/Robotic Mounts as Unit Members

Mount‑scale vehicles can count as **one or more Unit members**, bringing **vehicle DT/Flare** directly onto the front line. Their melee/ranged systems simply use larger dice and the same Focus/Spread chassis.

### Default Damage Assignment

Apply damage to the **lowest‑tier eligible targets** in range first (**rankers → elites; crew stations → officers**). Once per attack, a trait, or power may override targeting (“Pick the gun crew,” “Target the shield bearer”). This complements existing Protect/Defend tactical actions and shield tech.

### Morale at Formation Scale

When a Commander is downed, a Unit is destroyed, or scenario text demands it, trigger a **morale test** for that Formation. On failure, the Formation falls back or routs; a successful Rally may award **\+1 Command Die**. Uses the Guidebook’s morale frame as a baseline.

### Ship Combat (design notes)

These are reserved design notes for a future ship module polish and onboarding pass.

* **Vehicle/ship stat guidance.** Establish a lightweight stat grammar: *Size tag* (Mount/Light/Medium/Heavy), Officers & *Crew stations* (Gunnery/Helm/Damage Control/Boarding/Medic), *Arcs & Batteries* (e.g., Formation: “Port Battery: 4×1d12. 4x1d8”), *Hull DT & Flare* values, and *Specials* (Ram, Launch Bays, Tractor, Cloak, Mirror‑Plating, Keel Cannon).  
* **Boarding reactions.** Call out two naval reaction spends: **Repel Boarding** (Guard‑style Brace without forfeiture) and **Counter‑Board** (immediate move of a marine Unit to the threatened zone; mark and sacrifice next activation).  
* **Vehicle damage assignment nuance.** For vehicles, the default “lowest‑tier first” typically means **stations before hull**, **unless** an attack is explicitly hull‑targeted.  
* **Morale trigger (ships).** In ship actions, a **destroyed station** can also trigger a morale test for that Formation.

### Play Examples (design notes)

* **A. Countermove Costs Tempo.** Raiders surge; the defender spends **Receive Charge** on a Reach spear Unit to intercept and strike before contact. The reacting Unit is **marked** and later **skips** its activation to clear the debt.  
* **B. Volley Cracks Plating.** Two archer Units and one cannon battery all hit the same gunship this round. Spend **Volley** to sum into one source and beat **Hull DT**; **Flare** can only blunt **one** such big hit per round.  
* **C. Bodyguard Saves the Flag.** A Focused snipe targets the commander. **Bodyguard/Screen** redirects the single source to a nearby heavy Unit with DT 5; their shield then **Sunders** to cancel it outright.  
* **D. Cross‑Formation Deck Fight.** Boarders seize the port corridor. One Formation spends **Guard** to Brace the defending Marines without tempo loss; a second spends **Countermove** to slide a Damage Control Unit into the threatened zone (mark; skip next activation).
